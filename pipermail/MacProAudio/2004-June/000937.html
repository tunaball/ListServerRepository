A clumsy workaround is to create duplicate real instrument tracks (no
effects) and then drag the individual track there. Mute unwanted tracks and
export.

>
>Is Soundtrack a bit more complicated (ie, better) than GarageBand?
>Would it address my concerns above?

Soundtrack is strictly loops. No provision for recording, as far as I know,
though I haven't used it and I could be mistaken. No MIDI either. It does
support Quicktime video, and it has something GB lacks: Markers!

There are a couple of good books out there, and since GB is so cheap, it's
worth the investment in one of them. To get a good handle of all the iLife
apps, check out Jim Heid's iLife 04 book. If, like me, you're more attuned
to getting the most out of GB, I can recommend APPLE TRAINING SERIES:
GARAGE BAND by Mary Plummer (very detailed)$34.95 US, and GARAGE BAND
IGNITE! by Orren Merton at $19.95. The latter is a quickstart sort of thing
with lots of details, and each had info the other lacked.
