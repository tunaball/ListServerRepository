The real issue is the quality of the A to D converters Most brands of 
audio interfaces use an Analog Devices chip. So in spite of claimed 
differences a $99 stereo card is pretty much as as good as a $500 
stereo card.
If you have a good turntable pre amp hook it to a 24bit 96Khz interface 
and you are probably good to go.
If you are  really rich you can  spend thousands of dollars on 
"audiophile" gear for inperceptable differences in final quality.
But as an recording engineer for many years experience. Careful 
recording technique is worth a lot more than expensive gear.
Let  me recommend Audacity as a sound editor for restoring your old 
78RPMs it has a noise sampling and reduction effect ala Ray Gun and 
it's free.
