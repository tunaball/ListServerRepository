I have been using SoundDesigner2 (digi quit supporting it a LONG time 
ago) for years, and I bought Spark a couple of years ago.  I still 
use SD2, mainly because I know the program, but I've made a promise 
to myself to learn Spark this Summer and finally be digidesign-free 
once and for all!!!  It's got a lot more features than Peak, though 
both are fine programs for editing.  I don't know anything about dsp 
quattro.  Over the years, I've been doing more and more of my editing 
chores in Digital Performer, and about the only things I need a 
mono/stereo editor for are creating regions and re-drawing waveforms.
