Thubten,

SDII  =  Sound Designer II is a sound file format used on MAC systems.  For
example you have WAV files that Windows uses, etc.  Although the MAC can
record in other sound file formats, I prefer SDII. I may be just imagining
it, but to me SDII it is much richer (fatter) sounding than a WAV file, that
is just one of the many reasons I switched to MAC G4 dual 800 and Logic
Audio Platinum for recording. I have only had my MAC lock up one time and
that was my fault as I keep doing projects on my UW 160 SCSI drive without
ever de-fragmenting it, the HD, etc.  Well. even MACs need maintenance once
in a while, such as de-fragmenting the drives.  Also, I purchased a Oxford
911 120 gig firewire external drive to store song folders (files) on and I
now only have one song loaded and running on my SCSI drive at a time.  When
I finish the session.  I transfer the file to the Oxford 911.  As I said, I
still use my Windows based PCs but only for virtual synths like Gigastudio,
Wizoo's Virtual Guitarist, Spectrasonic's Trilogy (when it ships at the end
of this month).

Mike Knox
