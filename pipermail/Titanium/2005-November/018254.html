Well, perhaps, but in the meantime try this:
<http://www.google.com/search?hl=en&q=%22Anisotropic+conductive+film%22>

I've found there are two basic varieties of Anisotropic conductive film 
used to attach the circuit ribbons to the edge of an LCD, a thermosetting 
type and a thermplastic type. The thermosetting stuff must be removed 
(heat to loosen, then MEK to remove) and replaced to repair, but the 
thermoplastic type can be be reheated to 'reset'.

Dunno how you'd tell the diff, I guess just experiment . . . Anyhow, the 
google search above will provide an interested reader with hours of 
enjoyment. Did for me at least.
