Ralph Lewis wrote:

>
> On Feb 14, 2006, at 2:38 PM, Jim Freeman wrote:
>
>> I'm in the market for a screen protector for my 12" G4 powerbook.  
>> What do people recommend
>>
>> Background: my previous computer was a Pismo. I realized too late  
>> that my keyboard was making marks on the screen, and was never  fully 
>> able to clean them off.
>>
> I regularly clean the screen on my own and customers powerbooks by  
> dampening a soft cloth with windex and wiping the marks off. Works  
> beautifully. The marks are skin grease left on the keys by your  
> fingers. if left on the screen long enough they etch into the  
> surface. but cleaning once a month or so saves the bother of a cover,  
> and I have yet to see any damage to the screens from the cleaning

I found with Ti 400 that I didn't clean often enough, and so they did 
start etching into the surface. For this reason I asked a friend of mine 
who is a costume designed to cut up a piece of chamois leather to the 
dimensions of the keyboard. This made a perfect cover.

With my Al 1.67 I have no problems with marks on the screen whatsoever. 
I'm not using the cover. I am cleaning the screen regularly though.
