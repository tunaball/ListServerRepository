If the Ti still has AppleCare I suggest he uses that.

Everybody else having this problem with the Tibook should start by 
making certain that the top and bottom of the CDdrive is connected. 
When the Tibook gets older it gets wobbly and a gentle squeeze right in 
the centre of the drive will cause a snaping sound then when it 
reconnects. (Longfinger at the bottom and the thumb in the centre of 
the handrest on top will do it) After that it is time to go through all 
the little screws at the bottom and under the keyboard to get as much 
sturdiness as possible back to the Tibook. (and then check the squeeze 
trick again before loading the CD :-)) It also helps to use really thin 
CD:s to get the drive used to work again. Good quality music CDs is 
better then self burned once e.g.

My Tibook 400 has got so worn by now that I mostly has to do the little 
squeeze tric every time I'm going to use the CD/DVD-player.
