I bit last year and probably will this year too. Apple has a series of
freebies that make .mac somewhat attractive: things like StickyBrain, Marble
Madness, and a few other programs. A year's subscription to VersionTracker
is another. 

I use my iDisk to keep copies of my lessons and my students can access it to
get homework assignments, copies of lectures, and so forth. Parents like it
because they can see what assignments I've given and when the due dates are.
Between this, the web space, and email address I find it pretty valuable.
I've priced alternatives and I can get most of the same services for a
little less but I don't think it would be worth the hassles of changing.
