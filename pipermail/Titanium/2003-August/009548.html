On the subject of computer representation of numbers, I remember
seeing buttons at computer flea markets that said
 2 + 2 = 5
 for large
values of 2

This is easy to show if your calculator is limited to external
display of integer values but can carry additional internal precision.
If the internal value is 2.375, the most accurate external display
is "2".  If you add "2" + "2", you get 4.75 internally, or "5"
externally.

You can use this fact to nail ignorant pedants who rhetorically
ask, "Doesn't 2 + 2 equal 4?"
