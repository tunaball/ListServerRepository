InDesign 2 even allows you to use QuarkXPress 4.0 keystroke shortcuts 
(Edit > Keystroke Shortcuts ...opens a dialog box with many options). 
The Quark shortcuts for kerning/tracking and leading are so ingrained in 
me that I had to use this option.  It was a production life saver at first.

As I use it, I'm slowly customizing the shortcuts as I get more 
comfortable with ID2's way of doing things.  Also, it's so close to 
Photoshop and Illustator that I can usually fall back on what I know 
from those apps to figure out how ID2 works.  It's really quite easy, 
once you make the switch.
