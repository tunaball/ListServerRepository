I only use Apple power supplies (I've looked at the third party ones  
and they are no better imho) and I do have problems with them. I'm a  
heavy user and probably a bit heavy handed, constantly plugging in  
and unplugging and there are two main issues i've had:

a) Cable insulation wearing away and gradually the inner cable  
strands breaking.
b) Apple connector is poorly made, doesn't fit well, due to soft  
material it deforms further on use.

These (i hope) are now solved with the new magnetic connectors which  
seem like a much better solution.

My solution to these problems has been to count the cost of buying  
replacement supplies as an ongoing cost (like replacement batteries)  
to owning a Powerbook. I get through approximately 1 power brick per  
18 months.

Cheaper 3rd party supplies are definitely an option and I would not  
see any harm in using them as long as they are made to a comparable  
build quality.
