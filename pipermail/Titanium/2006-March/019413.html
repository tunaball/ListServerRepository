>> Should it be formatted for the Win machine? I'm assuming the flash

> The ones I've seen come formatted FAT32.  It'll work on Mac or PC.   
> If you want, you can format it HFS+ with Disk Utility, however then  
> it won't work on a PC.  Or you can format it NTFS on Windows, then  
> it won't work (it'll be read-only) on Mac.

I think this formatting was the source of some problems in my early  
experience with my  USB/flash-disk in a mixed environment.

I formatted it FAT32 on a PC, and then it works fine everywhere.

Office documents since 1997 are supposed to be compatible across the  
platforms.
I have no problems of incompatibility.

I regularly exchange all kinds of documents from PC/Mac/PC using my  
flash disk.
Now, it is seamless / transparent.
