Later, in Dubai Airport, when I got my Al-book out to use, I saw that 
the corner had been dented, and the aluminium above the DVI socket 
was sticking out about 5 mm.  I managed to bend it back in to 
position.  It does not look perfect, but the DVI socket - which I use 
all the time for giving my lectures with an LCD projector - is 
working fine.   I shall just live with my dent and slightly wavy 
frame around the DVI socket.  It is a reminder to always double-check 
the carrying strap clasp.  It could have been much worse.  This 
minimal damage, considering the energy abssorbed, shows that the 
Al-book is quite rugged and robust.

Rather like my (1997 vintage) 3400c, which in those days I dropped 
several times, once from about 1 metre on to concrete, and once from 
an overhead bin on a plane, with no effect....
