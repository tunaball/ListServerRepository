Sorry Trevor,

Though I am no pro either, I've worked enough with iMovie and iDVD to know
that 1.8Gb of free disk space is not nearly the minimum you should have
when you are working with video and film (even if it is only 20 minutes).

You should have at least 10 Gb of hard disk space absolutely free if you
want to work properly with film and video - the thing is, during the editing
and mixing phase, your computer can use up to 10 times the amount of disk
space than the original video/film file size occupied.  Also, when iMovie
imports .mov files, the imported .mov file can go from an original 10mb
file size to 200mb after it is imported!

I would suggest that you buy an external hard disk (at least 100Gb that
spins at 7200 rpms - La Cie make excellent ones).  Good luck and have fun.

Cheers!
