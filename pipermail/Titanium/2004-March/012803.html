I had an Epson printer that lasted all of 6 months before I finally got rid
of it. It was noisy, cheap in construction and slow. I forget the model
number (something like 420 - ?).

Anyhow, I moved to an HP 990cse and it was great! Heavily constructed and
quiet. The only problem I had with it was that the colour print heads kept
getting clogged and had to be replaced with half the ink still in them - And
they are expensive!

So I gave the HP away and moved to a 6 cartridge Canon i960. It isn't quite
the solid brute that the HP was, but it is quiet and blazingly fast and
produces awesome photos. I love the fact that if a print nozzle acts up, it
can be replaced leaving the other 5 intact. With the recent upgrade to the
print drivers it now is 100% compatible with Panther with all the functions
restored. My only complaint is that it doesn't come with a paper-saving
Duplex printing unit like the HP did, so I had to go out and buy one
after-market (which brought the price up considerably).
