On Feb 10, 2004, at 12:40 PM, Chris Olson wrote:

> Not the Titanium.  The 2002 circa PowerBook in question is limited to 
> 1GB because of the logic board chipset. The model in question was 
> introduced November 2002 and discontinued September 2003. It can 
> handle 1 GB via two 512 MB PC133 3.3v 144-pin SO-DIMM modules.
>

Theoretically, if a manufacturer were to make 1 GB PC133 3.3v 144-pin 
SODIMM modules, they would work in the Titanium.  I actually put 512 MB 
in a Wallstreet PowerBook that supposedly had a limit of 192 MB because 
at the time no one made 256 MB SDRAM chips.  I don't know if this will 
happen, and it probably would void the warranty since it would require 
getting to the "non-serviceable" RAM slot, but it *could* work.
