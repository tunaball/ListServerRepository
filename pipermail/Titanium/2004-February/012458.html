I concur with Dave; the battery is toast.
Lithium Ion batteries generally have a life of 2-3 years, depending on 
how much it is used (charge/discharge cycles don't matter as much as 
the number of electrons that flow in and out, and the amount of time 
that passes).  You're luckier than most people; most batteries when 
aging (of the 50 or 100 or so that I've had to replace and/or diagnose) 
tend to put the computer to sleep before they reach 0% charge; I've 
seen a few that are so bad they go to sleep with the percentage around 
80 or 90 or higher.  They then show 0% as normal when you plug it in 
and charge.  As far as I know, there's no external user-accessible way 
to tell the battery to rethink its capacity.

For most people, I would recommend purchasing one battery at a time and 
using it until it is dead, unless you have some dire need for more; for 
example, if you fly across the Atlantic frequently, and for some reason 
there aren't any power ports available.  Otherwise, the vast majority 
of people usually have some opportunity to charge up during the day.
