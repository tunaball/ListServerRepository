I'll weigh in with this: I love my 667, but Apple didn't put a simple 
circuit-breaker on the Firewire ports until they released the 800. I 
fried a port, and would need to do a motherboard swap to get Firewire 
back on the port. That sucks, big time. So watch the 667's, that's my 
advice . (I was using $40 cables too..)

Also, if you're thinking about upgrading to newer operating systems, 
keep a sharp eye on Video RAM. Anything under 64MB is going to be 
'semi-useful' when the Tiger comes out, especially regarding Video 
Units, or whatever they're calling them.  The 667s come with 16MB, 
and there is no upgrade path. Boooo!!!

My next Mac will probably be a desktop, but if I can swing a late, 
late model G4 Ti, I'll go that route as a happy second choice.
