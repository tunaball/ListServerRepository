ATA stands for Advanced Technology Attachment and is a method for  
connecting storage devices to computers using a parallel interface.   
(Serial ATA, or SATA, was introduced fairly recently and is a method  
for doing the same using a serial interface.)  ATA-4 is a version of  
ATA and refers to the the maximum data transfer speed capable on your  
ATA bus along with a few other factors such as type of transfer  
mode.  You can read about it to your heart's content at:

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advanced_Technology_Attachment
