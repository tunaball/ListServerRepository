At 8:04 PM +0000 2002.12.03, Doug Edwards wrote:

> have had one of the original TiBook since they were first introduced. I
>would say that the screen marks on the screen ARE due to finger grease
>transferred from the keyboard. However, if left, the grease will etch the
>soft glass used on the screen leaving permanent marks - in the worst case
>pits on the screen surface. If you see marks, clean them off immediately -
>but don't use solvents

We constantly hear of the marks being grease. But it seems to me that if the hard keys touch the screen closely enough to transfer grease, some incidental pressure could cause scratching or indenting, as well.

We keep explaining this and offering remedies from cleaning to rubber bumps. But after all this time you would think that customers would be warned in advance.
