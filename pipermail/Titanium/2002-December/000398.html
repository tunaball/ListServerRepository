On 2002/11/30, somebody, presumably Dan Zawadzki, mumbled out of the net:
>Does anyone know if there is a way to disable the embedded TiBook function
>key mappings for F1 through F6?  I want to use these keys in other
>applications but the Mac firmware always takes over and uses the default
>screen and audio controls unless I press the function key to null the
>default settings.

On 2002/11/30, somebody, presumably Charles J. Thompson, mumbled out 
of the net:
>Under OS X 10.2, use the Full Keyboard Access tab of the Keyboard 
>Preference under System Preferences.  Other OSes may vary.

It's whacky, but here's what worked for me. Boot the machine into Mac 
OS 9 with your Classic's System Folder. Then modify the settings in 
the Keyboard control panel to fit your taste. When you boot back into 
X, the fn keys will work, e.g., as DragThing hot keys.
