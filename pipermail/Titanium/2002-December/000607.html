I love microfiber cloths for this purpose as well (and a million others 
-- like cleaning/drying/polishing the cars).  You CAN dry microfiber in 
the clothes dryer with no problems as long as you do two things :  1.) 
put it in there by itself (I usually do a load of microfiber-only 
laundry) so it doesn't pick up lint from other stuff; and 2.) Don't use 
any fabric softener or dryer sheets


I love KlearScreen, but for day to day cleaning I use a dry or slightly 
dampened microfiber cloth -- does a great job for me.
