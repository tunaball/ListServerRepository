Aluminum, theater, center and color are U.S. spellings. British/Commonwealth spellings
are Aluminium, theatre, centre and colour (not to mention honour, valour, humour etc),
these last being via the French, who added the 'u' to them. Color, valor, humor, honor
are the original Latin spelling (3rd decl. nominative from memory). Theatre stems from
the Latin Theatrum, I think. Not sure about Aluminium and centre; don't have a Latin
dictionary handy.

I read somewhere that Noah Webster had the idea of simplifying the American spelling by
leaving out unneccesary letters, which has put Americans in the rather humourous
position of spelling 'haem' 'hem' (as in 'hem of a garment'), and, having left one
letter out of it, being forced to add another, vis 'heme', in order to avoid
confusion/absurdity. Funny...it's not even pronounced 'heme' in Latin; it's pronounced
'hyme'<g>
