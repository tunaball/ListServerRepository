Apple has Keynote, which has a lot of features that more than give 
PowerPoint a run for the money, and of course Address Book manages 
well enough, and then Mail is there, too. Address book and mail are 
freebies that work quite well.

I use Entourage, strictly for PIM and the addressing link to Word, in 
Office for Mac OSX. I also use Eudora, so Address Book, from Apple is 
not linked to my email, too bad, but i'm so used to Eudora's Address 
Book, I don't care.

One of these days, if i have what I overheard 'kids' referring to as 
a thing called 'free time', I'll write an Apple Script Studio routine 
that puts the same personal info/addresses, etc in Apple's Address 
book, Eudora's Address Book, Entourage, and Palm Desktop [for my Palm 
m130].. all at the same time. Now that would be grand.

Even though i am no fan of Microsoft as a company in our 'free' 
market system, I've used FreeOffice, and OpenOffice, and MS has [on 
the Macintosh/OSX platform] the Killer app. It really works, and 
AppleWorks, just the word processor, will never come close to Word, 
as it exists for the Mac, now, in the Office Suite. This isn't a 
'slam' on AW, or Apple, it's a simple case of:

There's Office for Mac OSX, and then there's everything else. Since 
about OS 10.1.2 or so, I have yet to see Word or Entourage crash, 
even once. Not many other applications can say that, not here in my 
setup.
