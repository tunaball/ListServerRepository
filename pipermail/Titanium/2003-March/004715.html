Tom - by far the best measure of the state of the battery is "minutes 
available when fully charged", because we have a target value for 
that for a "perfect battery" (eg 4 hours).

If the battery is in poor condition - as judged by that parameter - 
then I expect that it will also lose its charge quickly when unused. 
80% of charge lost in 10 days does not sound impossible for a poor 
battery.  But I do not have any data on that, as I never leave my Ti 
unused.......

Charge it up fully, then disconnect and see how many minutes you have 
left.  Let  me know what you find.

If it less than 30, your battery is in poor condition.
As I said, when my got below 10 then to 4, it was time for a new one.

You could fairly point out to potential purchaser of your Ti-400 that 
the battery is near the end of its life.    That would be a nice 
thing to do, as not everyone is aware of the 2-year life of a Li-ion 
battery.
