I can't speak for the 'audio trade', as a whole, but I use a MIDI 
in/out controller, and somewhat pricey software to get direct analog 
voice, as well as analog and digital sound,  'In', on a 667. I needed 
it to run synthesizers and a few signal processors [that were hanging 
around from the 'old' days], so, it was not much effort to use an 
external mic, also. MOTU's 828, and a little app called Mic Modeler 
[which is not necessary for general use] , do the job perfectly.

Aside: MicModeler uses patches to give an ordinary mic the same 
response curves of a wide variety of state-of-the-art recording mics, 
hence the 'not necessary' bit.

It may seem like a lot of 'work' or expense, but analog/digital 
converters and MIDI in/out boxes can be found, used, for not a lot of 
actual cash.
