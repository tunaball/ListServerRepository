The power jack on the DC board is probably broken, you just can't see  
it. There may be some electronic components on the DC board that got  
fried. Have you tried another power adapter, since maybe something  
blew up in the adapter and not on the DC board?

If the Powerbook can still run off the battery, your logic board is  
probably still OK. An out-of-warranty repair to replace just the DC/ 
audio board is about $330 (around $200 flat-rate repair, plus $100  
labor, plus tax). You can probably find used DC boards for around  
$100. To replace it, you'll need to be able to completely strip  
everything out of the bottom case.
