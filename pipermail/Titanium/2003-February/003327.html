Interestingly enough, in classical Greek, the word "tin" is a 
demonstrative pronoun roughly equivalent to "this" or "such a" as an 
emphatic, so "tin book" would have a double connotation (at least for 
any classical Greeks reading the postings) of not only book made of 
tin, but book being pointed out with special emphasis.

That being said, I don't think the new proposal is any more worthy of 
getting off the ground than Steve W's "mebook".  Powerbook or something 
plain vanilla and material-neutral probably has a better chance of 
lasting longer.  After all, do we want to change the name every two 
years as Apple works its way arond the periodic table?  (I don't want 
to know about Plutonium books!!)

Stephen
