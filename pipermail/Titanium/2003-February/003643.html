In time you'll see serious 'screen-redraw' issues popping up while 
running Quark in 'Classic' mode:

The main one being disappearing text lines, and 'boxes', during 
scrolling. Also, it's fascinating to see a gray-scale doc taking on a 
rust-colored 'vertical streaking' while scrolling. [A streaking which 
remains when the screen is stationary]. Slow reaction, on the part of 
the App/OS to 'mouse-downs'.

Of course these problems are intermittent [always the 'best' kind, 
no?]. And, over here, running latest 5.0.x release of Quark XPress 
Passport, we get the occasional crash [although nothing like running 
Quark 4.0.1 in OS 9].

If there is anything remotely resembling a 'deadline' in your 
work...stick with booting into OS 9.2. By the way, anyone who pays 
for the 4.1-->5.0 Quark upgrade, now, gets a 'free' upgrade to 6.0.
