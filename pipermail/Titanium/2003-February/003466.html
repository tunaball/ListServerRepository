> I've just this week switched to Mac's Mail (X) application, so I'm 
> familiar with many
> user errors.
> 1. Make sure your Internet connection is enabled.
> Test- launch Safari browser and turn to Google and do a new search
> (this guards against an old cached page appearing, if you have cache 
> saving turned on,
> which isn't very helpful with dynamic websites).
> 2. If you have multiple accounts, double check that the Account shown 
> for your outgoing
> mail is the account of your currently connected Internet Service 
> Provider.
> 3. Double-check Preferences Account info.
> It is easier to retrieve mail than to send it, in setting up Mail. 
> Some ISPs will not accept mail for sending if it is sent from an email 
> address not matching the ISP domain. Example, mail from 
> shakespeare at marslink likely won't get accepted by earthlink for an 
> upload (sending mail), although working through most ISPs, one could 
> download mail for shakespeare at marslink. Of course a third party, say 
> milton at aol.com, can use AOL to send mail to shakespeare at marslink and 
> that would go thru ok. ISPs apparently disfavor anonymous and 
> pseudonymous sent mail.
> 4. This creates problems when trying to reply to email lists that 
> restrict posting to email signups. Using one ISP at work and another 
> at home. Example, if original list (eg Ti) is subscribed thru 
> work at marslink, it can be downloaded and read via home venuslink ISP, 
> but attempted postings/replies from home/ venuslink will get bounced 
> from the list server (if preference email is work at venuslink; likely go 
> thru if "work at marslink". Thus, one's Preference "email" address may be 
> "mismatched" with "incoming mail server" to permit posting to 
> restricted lists.
