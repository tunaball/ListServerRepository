I feel your pain - to an extent.

But you aren't running OS X Server on your Ti, are you?  If Apple wants 
to compete head-to-head in the enterprise space, then yeah, they ought 
to try to deliver the kind of behavior you're looking for.

But you're not *just* running BSD on that TiBook and I think it is 
unrealistic for you to expect the same kind of experience you've had in 
the Unix world.  If you want to be able to patch your system and not 
reboot, you surely could accomplish that with a PPC variant of Linux, 
right?  :)  If not, why be surprised (or bothered) that you have to 
reboot when you patch OS X?

As far as running OS X Server on servers - I couldn't do it either - 
especially couldn't until they began offering a bundled hardware RAID 
solution.  Now that the XRaid is out I could consider it hardware-wise, 
but I'll have to see some performance and reliability reviews from 
people who run *real* production systems before I take a chance on it.
