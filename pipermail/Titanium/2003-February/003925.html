I've been using it since the beta and am quite happy with it.  It may 
take you a week or so to get used to using it all the time, but now I 
don't think I could live without it.  It's nice to allow different apps 
to have their own desktop to use so you don't have to keep maximizing 
and minimizing different apps.  It cuts down on screen clutter and 
their keyboard shortcuts to move between desktops are pretty easy 
(control + option + arrow key).  They give you quite a few preferences 
from which to choose, so you'll have to try them all out to see what 
would best fit the way you work.  I'd give it a try with their demo 
version.
