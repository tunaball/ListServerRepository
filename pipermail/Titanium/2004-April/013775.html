I'd bet that it's not particularly likely that it's wholly an OS  
problem; there are probably some other contributing factors.  USB  
devices, kernel extensions (Norton is *particularly* bad), etc.  Have  
either of you done an archive and install, and not installed any new  
items that require kernel extensions, or otherwise practice black  
magic?  I'd recommend removing ALL third-party devices before doing  
this; printers, RAM, etc. etc. etc., until you have run your computer  
for long enough to determine whether the problem is still present or  
not.
