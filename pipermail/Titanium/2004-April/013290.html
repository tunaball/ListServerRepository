I regret to be the bearer of bad news but I had a similar
problem when I "migrated" to the new AluBook. It appears that FCP 
installs machine specific ID files, meaning that it knows what 
platform it is installed on originally. I believe, that these files, 
although backed up, are looking for the ethernet# of the machine they 
were installed on to run FCP. 

This means a re-install of FCP. A further complication might be that 
AluBooks don't boot up in 9.2, so I don't know if you can preserve 
your favorites and preferences or even your third party stuff, although 
if you ran Classic from your backuped version it might work. 

Might be a good moment to go to FCP4 if you haven't yet. That's what 
I'm going to try. There's a very good FCP usergroup in LA: 
http://www.lafcpug.org  go on their forum and ask there.
