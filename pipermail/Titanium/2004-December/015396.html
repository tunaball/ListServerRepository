Hi,
I have this connection, it is , paradoxically, the only high-bandwidth
solution in regions of Germany that have glass fibre to the home (no ISPs
want to invest in the infrastructur to use these fibres).

> got amazing download speeds,
>but the upload would be dial-up/dsl

Yes, downloads are 80 to 120 kb/sec. Uploads depend on your POTS connection,
I have ISDN and it's 20 to 30 kb/sec.
DSL isn't possible in this scenario (because of the glass fibres, though
there are rumored to be local switches from fibre to copper wire for the
industry).

> ...which is not good, since you need a good upload bandwith as well (to
> get to larger download rates)

That isn't true in my experience, at least for big downloads. Might be
different for distributed download/upload situations like bittorrent or
peer-to-peer networks. I don't do that.
