>Why would you want to use a web browser for ftp? It's such a restrictive way to do it.

It is not a matter of 'Why' but a matter that many people need to do it. My company often sends an ftp link in an email for customers to download software. If the recipient's browser does ftp, then the customer is happy. If the browser doesn't do ftp, the customer sits looking at a blank screen or an error message and assumes we messed up. Many, many people don't know a thing about ftp clients.
