The original domain updating mechanism I was exposed to thru Netsol was horrible and was 
one of the main reasons I jumped over to dotster a couple years ago (but yeah, the drop from 
$35 to $15 made the jump all the sweeter). Now, the last two iterations of the Netsol 
management console are a gazillion times better than when I had my domains there. My main 
problem now is both my employer and my predecessor were sloppy record keepers, and had 
one Netsol account personna for each of the couple dozen domains we hold. I have had to go 
little by little and consolidating that gaggle of domains into one main account.

Steve and others have made a point, it is not just how much it is going to cost you for the 
domain itself, but how easy is it going to be to keep these records updated.
