No. You can set the resolution of your main screen and the projector

>This could explain why my Ti- desktop/ background was projected on 
>to the big screen.

No, that's because you weren't mirroring, so the projector was like a 
second monitor on which you hadn't placed any windows. See my 
previous message.

An iBook can only mirror, but on your TiBook the projector is 
independent from your laptop screen. This is really cool. For 
instance with KeyNote you can have the presentation on the projector 
in full screen mode, while the laptop shows the 3-panel layout with 
presentation, list of slides, plus your notes. That last feature is 
really great. You see your notes, the audience doesn't.
