Am Freitag, 16.05.03 um 11:20 Uhr schrieb Glenn Verhalle:

> are the keyboard and the Ram swappable between a 667 DVI (Second 
> generation) and a 1Ghz TiBook ?

As for the RAM, I think so. It should fit and work, though it might be 
slower (PC100 vs. PC133).

As for the keyboard, I doubt that you can swap them. I tried this once 
between a Rev B. and a Rev C. Ti and this were my results (posted last 
november):

> a week ago I asked this list whether there are any differences between 
> the keyboards of the various revisions of the Ti. Or if the keyboards 
> are exchangable. I didn't get any response so far.
>
> Now I got the keyboard I ordered via eBay and the answer to the 
> question above is negative. The keyboards aren't compatible. At least 
> not the ones of revision B and C.
>
> It is true that all keyboards are the same size and that the connector 
> is the same for all keyboards. But, the connector on the logic board 
> is at a slightly different position. So I was able to connect the 
> keyboard and it worked, but the cable was not long enough to bring the 
> keyboard in its position :-(
>
> This was my experience trying to connect a Rev C keyboard to a Rev B 
> Ti. The other way round the cable will be too long and probably the 
> keyboard won't fit either.

HTH,

	Marcus
