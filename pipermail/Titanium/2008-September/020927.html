> I have been using my 867 MHZ ti-book more on the road recently and  
> my battery keeps dying on me—it lasts about 3 hours max, just using  
> MsWord. I bought the laptop right when the aluminum Powerbook was  
> introduced. I can’t remember when that was, but it is the original  
> battery that I have. Two questions, can you recommend a back-up  
> battery? There seems to be a lot of them out there with differing  
> prices. The other question is: is there a way to recharge my laptop  
> battery through my car battery while I am driving?

Not sure about the battery, but to answer your second question, what  
you need is a car power inverter. Do a google search on that and  
you'll turn up lots of them. You should be able to get a good one in  
the $30-60 range. They plug into your cigarette lighter and convert  
the DC to AC. They typically provide one or two outlets, just like you  
would find in your house.
