>I know the iBook currently has an ATA33 2.5" HD and I am unsure if I 
>can use a ATA100 2.5" or if that is important or not.  I know 
>nothing about Hard Drives and wish I knew the difference between IDE

ATA and IDE are just different names for the same drive standard. 
Notebook drives are readily available at most computer supply 
sources. ATA100 will work fine.

Yes, you will need to add RAM.

Replacing a HD in a Clamshell is considered fairly challenging, even 
for experienced "case-crackers". Is there an Apple Authorized Dealer 
near you? They will often give you a good price on installing, if you 
buy the drive there.
