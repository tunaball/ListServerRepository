Unfortunately, this is difficult. None of the proprietry protocols want 
to encourage people not to use their client. All the protocols are 
different, and as they release new clients, they often deliberately try 
to break the 'free' clients.

'fire' (http://fire.sourceforge.net/) is a client that supports many 
different protocols, but unfortunately not for voice/video 
conferencing.

There is an open-source protocol called jabber 
(http://www.jabber.org/), but I don't know if it supports voice/video. 
Clients exist for most/all operating systems (Mac, Lin, Win at least). 
Fire supports Jabber.
