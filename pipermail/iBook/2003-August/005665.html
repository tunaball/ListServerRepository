This seems to assume that he is using "Connect automatically" setting, 
which he may not be using. Even still, it does not really make that 
much sense -- e.g. a 10 hour timeout? That seems extreme. Safari uses 
whatever the internet connection is. If there is no response from the 
server in 60 seconds, or if the server data transmission stops for 60 
seconds, it will timeout. Safari does not have the "power" to 
disconnect an internet connection, regardless of the connection method. 
Or maybe I am misinterpreting what Jack is saying?

The most important thing is to look at the connection log. SIGHUP is a 
hangup signal -- just as the "English" message indicates, the modem 
hungup; it can be due to local or remote issues. A verbose connection 
log might tell more of the story, but it is important to note what was 
going on at the time of the hangup ... because it might not. An 
unstable connection could be a "local" problem, it could be a problem 
at the ISP's end ...
