That would be somewhere around the 1400 mark. They all had 800*600 colour
screens, (The 'c' suffix denoted active matrix, the 'cs' was dual scan) they
had an expansion bay which could accommodate a CD rom drive.

My sister has one (hand-me-down from me), they are surprisingly robust. I
rebuilt the battery at some point, which is quite easy to do (unlike the
modern LiIon type cells). It now goes for about 3 hours between charges.

If you want to spend a little more, look for a 3400, they had the capability
to add USB and firewire (via the cardbus capable PC card slots). The 1400
had these slots too, but they were not cardbus capable, making Ethernet,
modem, flashcard readers etc the only useful cards for it.

One benefit the 1400 had, was that the processor card could be upgraded.
They are still making (I think) cards for it up to G3 @ 466Mhz!!

1400 had a ram ceiling of 64MB though... (3400 had 144MB Max)

Good luck with it,

Wiebe
