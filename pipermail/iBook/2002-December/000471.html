Actually, they did ... but it's still frustratingly slow for dialup 
users. Steve Jobs, like every other tech CEO in the country, figured 
incorrectly that a much higher percentage of the population would be on 
broadband by now. Notice how Software Update thinks nothing of trying 
to get you to download 35+MB files on a routine basis.

iDisk is generally acceptable on a cable/DSL connection ... not so 
great on dialup. To be blunt, you can expect more of this behaviour in 
the future, because the broadband numbers are finally starting to pick 
up (which will hopefully lower the cost at some point!).

Mac OS X is very much designed with the future in mind, and that future 
doesn't really include dialup. While I'm sure they will continue to 
support dialup as long as TCP/IP support is in there (ie for the 
foreseeable future), Apple along with most other tech manufacturers 
(and AOL, Earthlink, etc) clearly see dialup as the "floppy drive" of 
the internet. If you catch my drift.
