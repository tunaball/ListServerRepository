This is an easy question to answer in general. Just go back and look at 
Apple's quarterly reports for every quarter since the introduction of 
those models, find the number of iBook sales and add.

If you want to do it "quick and dirty," here's a loose take:

Number of iBooks sold in Q1 2003 (Oct1-Dec31 2002): 186,000
Number of iBooks sold in Q4 2002 (July1-Sept30 2002): 180,000
Number of iBooks sold in Q3 2001 (first quarter they were really 
available): 250,000

So let's project that Apple averaged 180,000 "icebook" style iBooks per 
quarter since they were released for sale in May 2001 (wow, was it that 
long ago?). Adding that up, you have 7 quarters x 180,000 (avg) = 1.26 
million. Call it 1.5 million by the time your product gets out there.
