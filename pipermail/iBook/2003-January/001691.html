On Monday, January 20, 2003, at 02:21  PM, Joe Dombrowski wrote:

> I can't remember which list this was posted on recently.  I need to do 
> a clean install of OS 10.1 on an iBook which I just sold.  I want the 
> new owner to start the iBook and be guided through the initial setup 
> just like a new Mac.  I seem to recall there was a particular 
> procedure to accomplish this.

A simple method, first I would suggest zeroing the drive using the Disk 
Utility. The zeroing is via an option in the Erase tab. This takes a 
while, but is as close as you can get to removing your personal data 
from the drive safely.

Then, just install X from your startup disks and when done, shut down. 
When they startup, there you are.
