The problem isn't the technology but legal issues--copyrights, import 
laws, etc. A piece of music may be copyright by different entities in 
different countries. There are online sites that sell Asian-made DVDs 
which are illegal to be sold in the US. These may even have been released 
by the original copyright holders (e.g., a US studio like Paramount), but 
labeled for sale only outside the US. The same applies to some music. 
Small, "fly-by-night" web sites and eBay sellers can get away with such 
dealings, but Apple couldn't. 

About two years ago, I found a source for videos of a British TV show 
that I liked. I contacted the publisher through their website, but they 
said that they couldn't ship the videos to the US, even in NTSC (vs PAL) 
format, because someone else owned the US rights to the show.
