Actually, it originates from the Baron von Munchausen. The Baron was told to
once have lifted himself from the quicksand by means of pulling himself up
by his bootstraps. The name bootstrapping was then used to indicate the
procedure by which a computer turns itself from a useless hunk of circuit
boards and wires into a useful computational device.

So, rather than "antagonistic" and "warlike", I'd say the term is humorous,
inspired, witty, and shows the literacy of the person that once canned it.

All IMHO, of course.
