Agreed, even if you do get into using the CMI, Cocktail is a great app. I
won't go without it either...

I would like to mention that before considering apps to make your new iBook
better have you considered adding an airport card? Maxing out the RAM? These
things will bring larger benefits than a few apps, IMHO.

I also happen to like a small hack for opening system prefs directly from
the dock to the area I wish to change without going through the show all
pane first called prefling.

Toast allows more flexibility for burning CDs/etc...

If you listen to internet radio a lot you might wish to consider Windows
Media Player for X or Real Player for X.

The great thing about the current situation with new Macs is that there is
nothing else really needed to make it run great...However, if you are new to
X there is a fantastic variety of possibilities open to you now...try
playing around with apples X11 and Open Office or using Fink to get to know
some great ports from Linux to Mac OS X...there are great exploration
possibilities for you as well with Java, Bluetooth, and Developer
Tools...also if you are interested in editing pictures then Gimp is
certainly worth checking out...and and and...
