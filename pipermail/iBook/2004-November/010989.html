Mine (600mhz late 2001) doesn't stay asleep if there's a Logitech mouse 
plugged in (maybe other mice too... that's all I've tried it with)... 
Logitech blames Apple, Apple blames logitech... I've never found a fix 
in two years except to unplug the mouse when I want to let the computer 
sleep. I got a replacement Logitech mouse (different model) and the 
problem persisted. I asked apple if there was a chance it was a logic 
board/IO bezel problem and they said there's no chance. Whatever. I'd 
buy another brand mouse, but I live in Japan, and Logitech (though it's 
called Logicool here) is absolutely dominant. Even the Apple Store 
doesn't sell Kensington here.

I guess the point is, make sure you have no USB devices plugged in, in 
case that's the cause.

Ben
