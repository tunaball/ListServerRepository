In the UK you can pay anything from £15 to £25 or more on DVDs that 
cost less in the US (ie, a bigger difference than could be accounted 
for due to the 17.5% VAT [sales tax] in the UK).

On average I've noticed that discs are £5 more expensive here than the 
equivalent in the US. If the price of a movie in the US is $15 then 
they seem to think it's ok to price the same disk at £17.99 ($27) in 
the UK. Almost all new releases here are priced at £20 ($30), apart 
from a few sure fire hits like Men in Black, LoTR etc which were £25.
