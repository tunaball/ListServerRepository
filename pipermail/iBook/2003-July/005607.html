I had to print out a rather funky Word document today -- heavy on 
tables, and several of them spilled into the margins. Since I was stuck 
in a meeting & bored, I ran it through several programs I had on hand...

AppleWorks 6 (came with the iBook) -- output garbled immensely.

OpenOffice.org 1.0.3 -- formatted well enough; required some tweaking 
but then the first table didn't print properly.

Word.X (on my desktop at work) -- not as good as one would expect; 
needed more tweaking than OOo but printed properly.

FrameMaker 7 -- took a long time to open the file, but required minimal 
tweaking & print was best of the bunch.

Just like the situations where a Mac does a better job of opening PC 
files, sometimes Word isn't the best choice to open a Word document. 
But if OOo did as well as it did with a "torture test" document, it 
certainly merits a little more prodding to see what it's capable of. 
Here's hoping for a Cocoa version, and soon!
