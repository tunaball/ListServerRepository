> putting it to sleep. I'm able to wake it up with the external 
> keyboard, but it goes back right away to sleep. To is annoying since 
> in order to use my external screen, the laptop screen must be opened !
>
> Any workaround or setting I missed ?

None that I'm aware of. While the later iBooks do have fans to keep the 
inner workings cool (the earlier clamshell models are entirely 
convection cooled), they're not designed to operate /completely/ 
closed; the build up of heat would cause component failures. (Many 
Powerbook models, though, can be operated while closed.)
