Cheap RAM often is not cheap if you factor in all of the problems it 
creates, the crashes, etc. All cheap RAM will not do this and the 
problem is not limited to cheap ram.

There seems to become a growing awareness that RAM is responsible for 
many problems users are experiencing. One proof is to remove the RAM 
and see if the problem goes away.

Before you purchase the ram, send an email to the company and ask if 
the RAM complies with Apple's specs for your particular book and OS and 
applications. Also ask if they will refund your money if the RAM fails 
to work properly and pass Apple's software check. Keep the email if you 
decide to purchase from them.
