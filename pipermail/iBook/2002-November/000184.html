I recently bought 2 Canon A2000 2 megapixels for $179 for student use.  They
are not fancy and don't have optical zoom, but the pics are very good - the
extreme close ups are better than my 3.1 Oly.  Canons take compact flash,
which is why I chose this camera over a similar Olympus which use smart
media.  Although I have no problem with sm and like the thinner profile, cf
is hardier for student use and is a few bucks cheaper generally.  SM though
has the gold contacts exposed - not for newbies who will thumb-print or
scratch them.  The sony's use memory sticks, which do not come as big as cf
and are more expensive.  I've heard the inexpensive Fugi's are getting quite
good, I think they take sm.
