In what sense do you mean "difference"?

When it comes to battery life (per the subject header), all three 
perform roughly the same.

The Icebook is easier to scratch and gets warmer IMHO (this is the 
500MHz combo drive Icebook BTW).

As far as speed, well, for such light use there's not really a 
noticeable speed gap between the clamshells and the Icebook when 
running OS 9, everthing just seems to be fast. Fast enough, I guess 
you'd say.

The chip speed becomes clear (along with the better, higher-rez screen) 
when you switch to OS X, though. The clamshells CAN run it, but IMHO 
not well. The Icebook runs it just fine.

Depends on your use. I tend to use laptops pretty lightly, I use my 
desktop machines for the real work around here. I take my laptops on 
trips to check mail, revise documents (mostly Word), and play simple 
games. Frankly, even a 300MHz clamshell is overkill for what I do with 
em.

One year I had to work on a full-page, full colour ad in Photoshop and 
Quark Express while on vacation. Without even thinking about it I chose 
the Icebook and man I'm glad I did.
