> Also space is an issue, the amount of data being burned shouldn't  
> matter, provided you have enough free space on the hard drive for  
> creation of the temporary invisible scratch file used during  
> burning. A good rule-of-thumb is to maintain at least as much free  
> space as the media to which you're copying data, plus 300 to 500 MB  
> for the Unix swap files in /private/var/vm. For a DVD-burner  
> equipped Mac, you'd want to keep 5 GB or more free space on the  
> hard drive; for a CD-R burner only Mac, you can get as low as  
> around 1.5 GB
>
