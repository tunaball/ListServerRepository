One point on types of cards.  CF is a great card, be aware however, that 
the majority of my repairs to Nikon, and other cameras that use this 
type of card is bent pins in the card reader in the camera.  I still am 
not sure how customers manage to jam the card in backwards, sideways or 
some way to bend the longer pins in the camera.  If they bend too far 
they will either blow fuses (Nikon, Fuji) or be bent so far over that 
they will break off if straightened.  I can replace individual pins on 
most models, however if too many are damaged the whole reader circuit 
may need to be replaced.  So use caution on how you insert the CF card. 

The XD card also has problems.  I have personally seen several where the 
entire contact surface has fallen off.  If you do not notice this, and 
stick it in the camera, it will jam.  It is virtually impossible for the 
customer to remove it.  The reader is not made to be disassembled; 
however, I have developed a method to repair them, IF the customer has 
not tried digging the stuck card out.  I think the contacts come loose 
due to the card being dropped.  So always check the XD card before 
inserting it in your camera.

Hope this helps someone.
