At 3:25 PM -0500 10/13/05, Scott Warren wrote:
>Could be the wep key stored on your laptop got corrupt.  You could 
>change the wep on the base station (suggested in another email 
>also), or you could delete your SSID out of the preferred networks 
>and re-enter it, or go into your keychain settings and delete the 
>airport parts...  I have seen that same error on a number of brands 
>of routers and I still have no idea what the root cause is.

As a general rule with wireless problems, try turning off any 
encryption and other security features (hiding the SSID, MAC access 
control).  If it works that way then reactivate the security, one at 
a time.
