> when you run "archive and install", are your applications and 
> preferences still in place and working?

Yes. Apple's bundled applications can be replaced with new copies if 
you specify this during the installation; the older versions are in a 
"Previous System" folder that's created as part of the process.

Users' folders are transferred over, preferences (which are in the 
"Library" folder) and all. The *System's* "Library" folder appears to 
be newly created, so you may have to reinstall printer drivers (as I 
did). But most everything else should be in your Home Directory.
