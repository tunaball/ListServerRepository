We had a situation around here (university Journalism program) in which 
a faculty member brought me a DVD-R that wouldn't run on a new eMac 
that we have for presentation purposes. I popped the DVD-R into my G4 
Superdrive and it read just fine. I popped it into my iBook with a 
combo-drive and it wouldn't read. "Huh?" I said with just the right 
amount of Ellen Feiss inflection, and then asked him what brand of 
DVD-R he used. "Some really cheap ones." he said.  I told him to try a 
better brand and see what happens. He also burned his DVD-R on his 
Phillips DVD burner that's hooked to his TV. Bottom line: see if the 
user is trying to get away with a cheap DVD-R before the poor machine 
gets blamed.
