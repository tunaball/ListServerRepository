In short, no.

Microsoft hasn't released a player for the Mac that supports the 
version 9 codec. Only the windows software can play these files.

The version 9 codec is the first step in their big DRM/content control 
nastiness, and it's unlikely we'll see this codec on the mac - why 
would they? If they get juicy exclusive media distribution deals 
they'll keep them PC only.

For the sake of my soul, I don't touch Windows Media Player.

I can do without any content that is in that format. It's not worth it.

You could ask your uni to use something other than WMP - like 
Quicktime, or even (shudder) Realplayer - at least Real Networks, while 
being a very shady/unlikable company, has a player that works on both 
Mac and PC with their latest codec.
