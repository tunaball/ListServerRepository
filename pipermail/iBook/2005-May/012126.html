On 28 May, 2005, at 5:07 pm, R. Hefner wrote:

> Does anyone know an easy way to remove all the excess languages etc., 
> basically anything unnecessary, in order to
> free up a bit of hard drive space?
> If it matters, I'm running 10.3.8.

Monolingual will remove unwanted language support. I had no problems 
with it, but see here for user feedback:

http://www.versiontracker.com/dyn/moreinfo/macosx/13031

On my iBook it freed about 400MB. I had missed the Customize button on 
the installer, so I ended up with about a gigabyte of printer drivers I 
didn't need. I just trashed them. I also got rid of the Tony Hawk game 
and, after playing with it a bit, Garage Band; the app itself didn't 
take up much space, but the software instruments and loops, which IIRC 
were in /Library/Application Support, did. All those combined left me 
with about four gigs of extra space.
