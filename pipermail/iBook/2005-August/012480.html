I have a Logitech Cordless Mouseman Optical.  It's a wireless two- 
button mouse with a scroll wheel, and it has a receiver that plugs  
into a computer's USB port.  I've never tried it with an iBook, but  
I've used it with my Power Mac G4 and it works great without any  
special drivers installed whatsoever.

-----------------
I'm using an iogear bluetooth mini mouse and love it.  Works great  
with my iBook, no drivers needed, and only a tiny hassle getting  
bluetooth to recognize it.  It has a usb tail that you plug in to  
recharge the batteries (included) though I usually keep a second set  
externally recharged. Not too horrendously expensive, but it was a  
touch more than most cordless since it is bluetooth.
