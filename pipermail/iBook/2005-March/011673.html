On 3-Mar-05, at 4:23 PM, Angus Wallace wrote:

> G'day,
>
> I've got an ibook, G4 800, and have had no problems. I do get the 
> feeling that apple improved the design/construction (in later models) 
> so that the logic board (etc) is not such a prob. I took mine around 
> Europe on a cycling trip last year, and I use it every day, and carry 
> it to work on my bike, ~20km/day, in panniers (i.e. bags on the back 
> of my bike). So far, it's worked flawlessly.
>
> I've gotta say, the ibook must be the most awesome computer I've ever 
> seen. What with its wicked sleep function, hardiness and reliability - 
> I consider it a bargain.
>
> I think they're very shock resistant, and that a some of the reported 
> problems are due to the way that people hold them. I often see people 
> carrying laptops with the screen open, and they hold them only at the 
> very front of hte computer - i.e. either side of the trackpad. This 
> introduces a lot of stress into the laptop, and it wouldn't surprise 
> me if that's the cause of many problems...  (design flaws aside)
>
> My recommendation is to close the screen when moving it, and carry it 
> under your arm like a book. ;-) If you must move it with the screen 
> open, put your hands right underneath, so that it is balanced on your 
> hands. This will introduce much less stress.
>
> HTH,
> -Angus
>
> ps. I'm not suggesting that everyone/anyone who's had problems has 
> mistreated their ibook, but that there are patterns of usage that 
> could perhaps exacerbate a design flaw.
>
