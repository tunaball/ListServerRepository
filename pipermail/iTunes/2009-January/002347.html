Now, how many subscribers to satellite broadband in the US? To find  
out, I went here:

<http://satellite.tmcnet.com/topics/satellite/articles/33624-satellite-broadband-prospects.htm 
 >

and found the following statistic:

		Assume 90 percent of the subscribers are in the U.S. market, meaning  
there are about 540,000 U.S. 		
		consumer broadband (satellite) subscribers.

The ratio of satellite subscribers (540,000) to total broadband  
subscribers (100,000,000) is therefore 0.5% of total.

So in spite of your allegation that "more than 1% of the national  
population lives in rural areas.", it turns out that only 1/2 of one  
percent of total number of US broadband subscribers have satellite.  
Even if ALL satellite companies capped downloads except during certain  
hours, (And we don't know if that's true, some may not do so) my  
statement that "Less than 1% of US subscribers would need such a  
feature on iTunes as a result of their Satellite subscription  
policies" would seem to be correct.
