When you rip an audio book you might try using AAC* format rather than  
MP3.
I join all the tracks on a CD by selecting all the tracks and choosing  
"Join
Tracks" under the Advanced Menu.  I then find the files in the iTunes  
music
folder and change the suffex from m4a to m4b.  This makes them  
bookmarkable.
I then reimport them into iTunes and they are listed under Audio  
Books.  Now
if I stop listening the computer or iPod remembers where I was when I  
get back
to the book.  This might solve your play count issue also.
