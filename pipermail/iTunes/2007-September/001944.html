Even Macs are happier with some basic maintenance procedures, two of 
which is to do disk repair and repair permissions occasionally using 
Disk Utility. To easily deal with corrupt preference files, which are 
not all that uncommon, try Preferential Treatment. It verifies 
preference files and identifies and isolates corrupt ones. You can get 
it at versiontracker.com.

Something even better is AppleJack. It performs 5 maintenance procedures 
sequentially:
1 Repair Disk
2 Repair Permissions
3 Verify Preference files
4 Empty caches
5 Deletes Swap files

Read about it and download it from here <http://applejack.sourceforge.net/>
