"Internet & Network" no longed exists in 10.3.5.
Go to the preferences in Safari and select it as your default browser.

>TDavis8810, sounds to me like you need to open System Preferences, 
>then in the SP window under the "Internet & Network" heading click 
>"Internet." In the "Internet" window that opens up click on the 
>"Web" tab, where the choice of default browser is offered. The 
>drop-down browser window will either show Safari as an option, which 
>you should highlight, or you'll need to click on "Select," which 
>will open a navigation window allowing you to find Safari on your 
>hard drive (probably in the "Applications" folder) and select it. 
>Drag the Safari icon to the dock for easy access. Then when you 
>click on the icon, Safari should open, provided you've selected it 
>as the default browser in the System Preferences "Internet" window.
>
>Concerning Apple Mail drag the Mail icon to the dock, where you can 
>then easily open it. When you want to check email, click on the dock 
>Mail icon, opening the Mail application, which should then download 
>your mail from the AOL server.
>
