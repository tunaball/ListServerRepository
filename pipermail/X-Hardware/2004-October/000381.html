It's a very chatty broadcast protocol, isn't supported at layer 2 by a 
lot of equipment, and doesn't scale well across enterprise LANs - much 
like Novell and NetBios (old Windows protocol).  I'm not surprised 
they're trying to standardise infrastructure on TCP/IP, there are many 
good reasons for doing so.  I just don't understand how going to OS X 
is going to accomplish that.  Both OS 8+ and OS X support TCP/IP, 
Novell, and AppleTalk AFAIK.
