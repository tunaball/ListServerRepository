> I had an external Firewire hard drive that did the same thing. I could

I couldn't tell if you've done this yet or not, but before going that 
far I would make sure you try a different cable and try both ports on 
the drive (assuming it has two ports). I have an older drive where one 
of the ports just doesn't seem to give me a good connection, while the 
other port works fine.

A couple other things I usually pay attention to when a drive doesn't 
mount :

Is the power light on and steady (not flickering)?

When it starts up, do you hear the drive spin up?

When its finished spinning up, do you hear a quick read or two occur (a 
couple little "scritches")?

Sound can be a great diagnostic tool, so I like to know how my hardware 
sounds when functioning properly, wether its a car or a hard drive.
