Think of it as a standard, like the accelerator being on the right of 
the brake. It might seem nice to have things customizable without 
limit, but in practice, its not a good idea. Its a standard that makes 
things a lot easier if anyone else ever needs to use your system or 
figure out what's wrong with it, or write an installer that works on 
your system. Its a standard that's much bigger than Mac and much older 
than windows. Welcome to a real computer.

If its any consolation, some of those folders (etc, mach,  . .) are 
hidden in OS X.
