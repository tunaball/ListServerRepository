You may have read the article, but you did not comprehend.


  Only one of the machines has to be capable of Auto-MDIX. There are 2 
lists in the article.
The second list, which contains those which are not auto-capable, says 
that 2 products on the 2nd list need a crossover cable when connected 
together. It does not say a product on the 2nd list needs a crossover 
cable to connect to one on the first list.

This is the key part of the article:

> Auto-MDIX automatically switches between MDI and MDI-X, which allows 
> either type of cable to be connected regardless of whether you are 
> connecting two computers directly, or you are connecting to a hub or 
> switch.
>
> Note: MDI devices connected to Auto-MDIX devices do not require a 
> crossover Ethernet cable.



Auto-MDIX are the new machines. MDI are the old machines. So that last 
line could read:
Note: Old machines connected to new machines do not require a crossover 
cable.
