Not everything works all the time; especially for neophytes.

You tried those solutions (including resetting the NVRAM, I assume) and 
still can't boot off the hard disk? (Presumably, you have only one? -- 
You were very spare with specific details.) Then boot off an alternate 
drive, i.e., your CD-ROM drive. Use the OS X CD, the OS 9 CD, the 8.6 
CD that came with your B&W, whatever. Success? -> You have a disk 
corruption problem. Use a disk utility to try to fix it, re-install the 
OS, or (what may very well be best in the long run) reinitialize the 
drive and restore everything from the backup. Failure? -> You have a 
hardware problem. You need to roll up your sleeves and crack open the 
candy box.
