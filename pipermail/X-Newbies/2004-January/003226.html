I'm sure about TextEdit. However, you are missing one point about
AppleWorks: people like me who have OLD iMacs (a 350MHz blueberry slot
loading) got AW5, and many of us never upgraded to 6. It can read old .doc
files, but not some newer ones.

Good news is, with lots of memory, this old iMac works *fine* with OS X
10.3.2 Panther. I had to get Office X for work, but I understand why people
want to avoid it. One big issue with Office X is that it is *not* a Cocoa
app but a poor Carbon version, so it does not allow Services in OS X. That
is a real limitation when you get used to using a large number of services
in X.

I think that people who rarely need Office would do well to keep their old
Classic version of office just for compatibility purposes and use AW,
Mariner, Nisus, etc.
