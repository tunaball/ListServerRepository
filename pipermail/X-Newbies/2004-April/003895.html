There is no inherent right not to be offended. Taking offense precludes
examining the issue. A secure stand on an issue can include tolerance of
other views. I've been around awhile: was a child in the repressive,
hypocritical 50's, came of age in the more permissive (and later
self-involved) 60's and 70's. I still clearly remember the divisive,
condemnatory period of Viet Nam, during which many patriotic, idealistic
Americans were labeled "unpatriotic" cuz they disagreed with a misguided
power structure. I've seen the debate of political correctness vs. free
expression. I've learned this: you can't really change someone's mind or
heart. Only they can do that.

A lifelong liberal with some conservative positions, I loathe to the core
the current state of talk radio, a medium I used to find stimulating. It's
degenerated to undisciplined thinking (if at all), a hotbed of a priori
assumptions, the lowest common denominator. Heat gets ratings, while light
does not. But I value the system that enables these morons and demagogues
to preach to their respective (though disrespectful) choirs. I prefer
discourse, but I always have the option of changing the channel.

See you your own freedoms by guarding the rights of those with whom you
disagree (like those who love OSX, for example. See? It' IS on topic).
Examine your own tendency to be offended from a standpoint of your own
responsibility. They're your feelings, after all. When you limit another's
free expression you threaten your own. The winds of change blow from any
direction they wish.
