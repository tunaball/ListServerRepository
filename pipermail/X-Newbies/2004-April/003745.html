That judgment was absolutely correct at the time it was issued (1998), 
but does not presume to be true forever. Likewise, the finding of the 
courts regarding Microsoft may not be true forever either, but they are 
certainly true today. If Microsoft goes out of business, is it still a 
monopoly? No.

Judgments establish facts for use in determining proper enforcement of 
the law, as you yourself quote above. They are applicable at the time 
of the judgment and for as long as the conclusions stand, which is not 
and has never meant to be for all time. That's why we don't write 
judgments in stone. :)
