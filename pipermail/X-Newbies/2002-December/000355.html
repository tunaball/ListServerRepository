A couple ways you could handle this:

- Leave the Full Name field blank and enter something like this in the 
Email Address Field:

John Doe <jdoe at apple.com>, Jonathan Doe <jdoe at mac.com>

- Leave the second account always configured, but either deactivate it 
or turn off automatic fetching for the second account (both prefs are 
in the Advanced tab).

>> The support is there on the Mail side to do this via Applescript.
>
> No, it will import messages and calendars, but not the accounts, rules 
> and signatures from Entourage... I've tried it numerous times.

Via Applescript, only, not using the Import Mailboxes command. Sadly, 
most other email clients aren't scriptable enough to support scripting 
the data from rules and signatures. So, Mail has the support to do this 
via Applescript, but it's not as useful as it could be due to the lack 
of support from other clients. Doing such an import would involve some 
reverse engineering of proprietary data formats.

Address Book will also import vCards (via drag and drop) if Day-to-Day 
supports vCards. There is no generic tab-delimited importer in Address 
Book or the script.

> No, when I move a great number of messages from a folder to another

> messages" box) Apple Mail will slow down to a crawl and typing will be 
> jumpy while the message "indexing message 189 of 3578" appears in the 
> status bar. I have almost 2 Gigs of messages, remember....

You are probably pushing the limits with 2 gigs of email. Other than 
keeping more mailboxes with a smaller set of messages (and using 
multiple selection if you want to see multiple mailboxes at a time), I 
don't really have a good suggestion here other than trying out a more 
high-end email client like Mailsmith or Entourage.
