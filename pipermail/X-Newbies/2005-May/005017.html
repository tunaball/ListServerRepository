You have to understand what S.M.A.R.T. is and what it isn't. It's not a  
comprehensive disk testing system; essentially, it's a means of asking  
the drive if its firmware thinks it's healthy. Current standards don't  
require more than a Yes/No type of answer. The firmware may be able to  
provide more data, but your software tool must know how to ask and how  
to interpret the answer.

For more details, see

<http://www.storagereview.com/guide2000/ref/hdd/perf/qual/ 
featuresSMART.html>

and for even more details, try

<http://www.linuxjournal.com/node/6983/print>
