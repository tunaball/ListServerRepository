True. When you get down to it, the harder a post is to read, the less
likely the poster is to receive a helpful reply (or even get his post
read in the first place).

I humbly suggest:

A) Ignoring or killing (filtering out) posts/threads that are annoying,
of no interest or simply boring in some way.

B) If you do want to reply, use liberal "[snip unwanted content]", to
make the reply more palatable than the original (i.e. don't perpetuate
unwanted, or unneeded content), and maybe put your feelings into your
sig (see mine below for an example of an anti-top-posting sig).

C) If you're the one who breached netiquette - just bite your tongue and
be glad anyone bothered to reply at all. Or ignore and/or kill replies
you find annoying. Or reply and have your say, if you really want to. If
_we_ don't like your reply _we'll_ ignore/mark it read/kill it. It's no
big deal, and not worth getting bent out of shape over.
