> How do I manage my web images?
If you are not building a website and only want to download images to 
your desk top, "control key" + "mouse click" on the image and choose 
"download image to".  You can download to your desktop or to a folder 
you have made up in your "users".

> How do I "Slide Show" view jpg.s and other saved images?

iPhoto has the option for slide show second icon after print on the 
bottom of the window.

> I tried a slide show in the Graphic Converter, there has got to be
> an easier way.

> I like iPhoto. I can't seem to get them into there.

If you wish to use iPhoto to view your pictures, you can choose it to 
be the default application to open pictures. Or just open iPhoto and 
click import and it will import from your camera.
again, click on import and choose pictures you want to import

> I tried to import them with iPhoto it didn't work.
what did you do exactly?  Import is under File
>
> I view images with preview and it doesn't put
> any of the other pictures in the draw.

Sorry, I don't do windows and don't know what is a draw?
>
> I will expand on exactly what I do with "Preview"
> 1. I click on one photo in the folder
> 2. The photo comes up in Preview
> 3. The other pictures in that folder do not show up in the drawer
>
> Please tell me the best way to manage my jpg.s

Preview is not the application of choice IMO.  Use iPhoto to view and 
use Graphic Converter to edit as it has more editing options.  You can 
save your pictures to a "new folder" in your user documents.  From 
there you can "open" from the applications or "import" or just drag and 
drop in whatever application you wish.  Hope this explains further.
