I forget know your version of OSX (Tiger?) , but on the original disks
that came with the Mac (either: Jaguar or Panther) you'll have the
Applworks package, and with it's path already discussed, simply insert
the disk and then startup Pacifist and show it to the disk and follow
the path to the file (.pkg) and let it be pulled from the disk to your
desktop, when that's completed quit Pacifist. By now, you should've
already Repaired Permissions, so a doubleclick on the Appleworks.pkg
should start the Install application, so let it do it's job, and
assuming you've Admin rights you will probably need to supply your
password for the install to continue, and after it has finished
restart your Mac and Repair Permissions once again, now a trip to:
<http://www.apple.com/downloads/macosx/apple/appleworksformacosx.html>
and download of the correct updater (per your language used) to your
desktop with subsequint doubleclick install, and once more again a
Logout/Login or restart completed and then Repair Permissions done,
should have you back at a useable v6.2.9 Appleworks.

The ABC's are purty much easy and common logic stepped patterns,
but reading of ReadMes and all connected Documents is a must and
highly recommendable, and by doing so will more than likely save
you lotz of time and possible heartache. Goodluck!, and do let us
know how your adventure has faired in the end.
