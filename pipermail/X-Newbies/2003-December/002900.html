> If you upgrade to Panther, it shouldn't. It might be
> that
> you need to recompile some stuff to get it to work,
> but
> as a general rule this shouldn't be required.
> 
> But, based on experience, I would recommend to at
> least perform an Archive and Install. I've done 100+
> "upgrades" from 10.1 -> 10.2 -> 10.3. And, doing the
> upgrades has given me flaky results many times.
> I thus prefer the "clean upgrade" solution you get
> with either an Archive and Install or format +
> install.
> 
> But, alas, YMMV :)
