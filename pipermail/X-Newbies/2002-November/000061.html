It was my understanding that *any* bootable operation system had to be 
on the first 8Gb of the drive. That would include OS9. If I am wrong, 
and booting 9 from beyond the 8Gb mark is possible I'd really like to 
know about it.

My partition scheme has been ( 1998 Rev A iMac - 40Gb HD, 384Mb ram, 
6Mb video ram) 1Gb for OS9, 6.9Gb for OSX, and the rest for Data such 
as Mp3s, pictures, OS9 applications, and all other non-OSX 
applications. It seems to work well and browsing the OSX partition is 
nice and clean, that is, free of OS9 clutter.
