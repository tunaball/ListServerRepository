--- Robert MacLeay <robertmacleay at mac.com> wrote:

> Yes, with a big gotcha...
> 
> The second drive must have booting supported.
> 
> For instance, if both drives are internal to your
> Mac, no problem.
> 
> But if the second one is an external USB drive, you
> can clone it
> successfully, but be unable to boot because
> pre-Macintel Macs simply won't
> boot off any USB drive, no matter what is on them.
> 
> You could, however, remove a cloned drive from its
> USB enclosure and install
> it inside your Mac, and boot off of it. I have done
> this.
> 
> Finally, investigate SuperDuper! as an alternative
> to CCC;
> It offers everything CCC does and more, especially
> for incremental backups.
