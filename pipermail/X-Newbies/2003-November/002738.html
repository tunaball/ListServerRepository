Tracy, first open AppleMenu/AboutThis Mac/ SystemProfile and see what 
kind of drive you have now.  The newer G5 macs have a different drive 
(Serial ATA) as opposed to the older macs.  http://eshop.macsales.com 
is a good place to buy online.  Most people I have talked with like IBM 
or Maxtor drives, although my daughter's Maxtor died after 1 1/2 years. 
When you got your computer you may have gotten 4 little screws that you 
hid somewhere.  Or you can use the screws that are holding in your old 
drive.  Your manual should tell you how to install the drive.  You can 
buy a bigger drive than you currently  have, depends on how much you 
want to $$$$.

I should ask if you have run some utilities on your drive like Norton 
or Tech Tool or Disk Warrier.  Maybe it is fixable  In order to run the 
Utilities properly, boot up from the CD and chose the HD to fix.
