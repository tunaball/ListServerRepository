AOL users should also consider letting AOL know that it's time they 
supported MIME. It's been a standard for nearly 7 years now and every 
email client currently in production has supported it for at least 5 
years.

It's not some weird fringe format that they can continue to turn a 
blind eye to.

Point them to this URL:

http://www.ietf.org/rfc/rfc2045.txt

As an email client developer, we can work around a lot of things in 
other programs, but it makes it very hard when AOL considers 
interoperability to be something to deny its users for no good reason.

There's even freely available source code that shows how to implement 
MIME in an email client here:

ftp://ftp.cac.washington.edu/imap/c-client_archive

Don't be afraid to complain. You're paying them good money for a 
service and it should give you at least basic functionality (like 
receiving attachments).

- cricket
