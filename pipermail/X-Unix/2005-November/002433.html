This kind of stuff is fun for me, here's something I whipped up...
____________
#!/bin/bash

printf  "I will add \"$1\" to the files, continue? [y/n]: "
read a

case $a in
    Y|y)
        #command to execute goes here
        ;;
    *)
        exit 0
esac
___________

The first line tells the script to use bash, even though your default shell
is something else.
The printf line echoes the email address you entered as a parameter, i.e.
$ addname.sh me at privacy.net
Would give you
$ I will add "me at privacy.net" to the files, continue? [y/n]:
You would then press "Y" or "y" to run the script, or anything else to exit.
Simple.  ;-)

but that doesn't present too much of a challenge.  Maybe you could let me
