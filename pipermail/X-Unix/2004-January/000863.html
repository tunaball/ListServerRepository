	Short ago I posted a couple of questions to this list regarding man 
pages in Panther, but I didn't get any answer... probably an 
understanding of my problem would have helped me answer your man 
related questions. It looks like I'm going to have to post again :-) In 
any case, there's this sweet trick in the man(1) command that I think 
very few are aware about. Do you like PostScript and/or PDFs? Well, it 
turns out you can generate PostScript pages out of man pages with 
nothing more than the simple man(1) command! What page do you want, you 
want to read the bash man page? Well:

man -t bash > bash.ps

	If you distill that to PDF with something like Preview.app or 
TeXShop.app you'll get a nice looking PDF of the bash man page. Sweet, 
aint it?

	Hope that helps for the moment. I'll be reposting my man questions 
soon so you might want to look out for them. Regards,...
