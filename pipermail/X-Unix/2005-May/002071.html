Since it's "superuser do" then Sue-Due makes a lot of sense, but I always assumed this was a pun .. instead of s(etting) u(ser), you're just pretending, for a moment, to be someone pseudonymously (I'm sure there's a word in there somewhere), without changing whoareu, so to speak. Otherwise 'dosu', 'suno', or many other letter combinations, seems more apropos. In other words, even if it isn't "pseudo", it ought to be, so that's what it will always be to me.

The truly curious could probably just ask the authors:

but if they say it isn't "pseudo", then they're probably wrong :)
