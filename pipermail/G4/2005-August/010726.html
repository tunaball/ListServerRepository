On 1 Aug, 2005, at 9:27, dapper dan wrote:

also,how much bigger an internal hard drive can i throw in a g4 
powerbook 867mhz powerbook?i have 30 gig.is 80 possible? any advantage 
to an internal over external one?one more question.can i buy an 
internal/external drive that spins at a higher rate than the one that 
came with the powerbook?

1. You can put in as large capacity a drive as is made and you can 
afford as long as it will physically fit in the powerbook.
2. Internal drives will be able to boot the machine, external drives 
may not be able to boot (USB cannot boot, but FireWire can).
3. An external can be either slower or faster, it depends on the speed 
that you buy.
