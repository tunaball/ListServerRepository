Apple claims that AAC is a more efficient coding algorithm than MP3.  
I.e., AAC @ 128kbps is better than MP3 @ 160 or 192 kbps.

There was an article on it somewhere.  The interesting point I found 
was that the reviewer claimed that using QT to encode music gave a 
better result than iTunes (Yes, I know they use the same library - but 
they DO NOT apparently use the same settings).

M4A and M4P are identical except that the M4P is PROTECTED - e.g., you 
can not send these to your friends - you must be authenticated as they 
are part of a content protection scheme.
