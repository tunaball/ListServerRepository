Hi guys.

My learning curve on OS X and local networks is increasing apace. My 
two G3s are now talking away happily to the G4 and v.v.

As I am sadly without the possibility pro tem of broadband out here 
in the sticks, I purchased and downloaded some nifty and cheap 
software called Onspeed, which does exactly what it promised and 
speeds up OS X downloads in Safari and Explorer dramatically. They 
are apparently coming up with a similar speed-up for Mac email in a 
couple of months, plus a few other goodies.

They also have a real live customer support facility available via 
iChat, which proved invaluable when I messed up the final stages of 
installation by failing to RTFM. Ten minutes of chat, and all was 
well again.

Thoroughly recommended as a halfway house to full broadband, and a 
lot cheaper at Euro 39.99 p.a.

See:

http://www.onspeed.com/
