--- John Niven <senseamp at yahoo.com> escreveu:

> Francisco,
> 
> I don't think anybody has addressed your question.
> 
> I doubt you can use a QS dual processor in your AGP.
> 
> The first question is can you run a dual processor
> with your motherboard. You have to check the
> revision
> using this app: 
> 
>
http://www.macbidouille.com/downloads/UniNorthASICChecker.sit
> 
> It will let you know if your motherboard supports
> dual
> processors.
> 
> The second factor is physical. The motherboard cpu
> connector is the same, but the daughterboard is a
> different shape. You would have to be able to solder
> and hack the heatsink.
> 
> I have an older AGP that wouldn't take a dual
> up-grade
> so I fitted a 733MHz single from a QS. I just
> removed
> the ata dvd interface and used the main one instead.
> I
> use SCSI drives anyway. I then resoldered the speed
> configuration resistors so that the cpu ran at
> 800MHz
> with the slower 100MHz bus. I hacked some of the
> fins
> off the heatsink so the case closes.
> 
> It works. Runs Tiger. I use it at work. But I
> wouldn't
> recommend it. Faster Macs are to be had for not much
> money, and no effort/risk.
> 
> If you CAN run a dual cpu then look for an original
> Apple dual 450 or 500. That's an easy, cheap, plug
> and
> play upgrade.
> 
> To make your mac run faster:
> 1) get at least 1Gb of ram
> 2) fit a modern ATA harddrive with a big cache and
> 7200rpm.
> 3) get a modest video card upgrade, that will run
> quartz extreme. Flashed PC version on ebay work.
> 4) Relax. What do you expect from a 7 year old
> machine
> :-)
>
