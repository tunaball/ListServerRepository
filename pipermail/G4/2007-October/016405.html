If it's a G4 it takes ATA drives. As mentioned the connectors are on  
the motherboard and if it doesn't have the cable it is a standard  
item in stores.
You can get a SCSI card if there is some reason for YOU to use a SCSI  
drive, but for the boot  drive at least, you want the regular  ATA  
drive like they sell everywhere.
And the SCSI info that you posted as relevant to your drive and it's  
connection was relevant years ago. There are NO SCSI connectors in  
G4's and forcing it to eat SCSI for breakfast is just more trouble  
than it is worth.
