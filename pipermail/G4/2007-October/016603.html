Hello Rick:

I also have the Quicksilver G4/733.  You are looking at a very  
interesting project especially if your machine is already pretty well  
souped up with RAM and hard drive(s).  After doing your homework for  
the upgrade and adding up the costs of buying Leopard, the CPU, and the  
video card, and any other upgrades, you may want to compare that to the  
cost of a new or refurbished Intel iMac.  And you might scratch up a  
few dollars by selling your old machine.

You could also look at the Mac-Mini, especially if you have a monitor  
that does not have an ADC connector.  If you have an Apple LCD display  
with the ADC connector, that would require a DVI to ADC adaptor for $99  
to work the Mini.
