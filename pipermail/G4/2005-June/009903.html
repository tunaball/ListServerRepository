Sodium bicarbonate and carbonic acid (baking soda) are well known as cleaning agents. They are regularly touted in newspaper columns - Heloise.  The test is in the residue. Sugar is terrible, donno about caffeine. Diet Coke? Uric acid is also a good cleaner but I really don't think aiming at a keyboard is a good idea. Save it for putting out the campfire.

Don't forger WD-40. The WD probably stands for "water displacement". I have used it on sticky switches with much success. It's not a lubricant but it does move existing grease around with a rejuvenation effect. It seems to be OK for use on electronic plastics.
