On Jun 23, 2005, at 10:26, Tony Gamble wrote:

> On 23-Jun-05, at 10:08 AM, Alex Hurst wrote:
>
>> BTW, I'm slightly confused on Cat 5 and crossover cables. Apple 
>> recommends a crossover cable when linking two Macs directly together. 
>> I assume that this doesn't apply when using a hub?
>
> Yes, crossover cables are the standard when linking two machines 
> directly for communication.  Some of the newer Macs, however, have a 
> "smart" ethernet port that automatically configures itself 
> appropriately regardless of the cable used.

(1) "Cat 5" is a cable standard. "Crossover" refers to the cable's pin 
wiring.

(2) Apple doesn't recommend crossover cables for patching two Macs 
together. It states that some products require crossover cables, while 
others don't. See

<http://docs.info.apple.com/article.html?artnum=42717>
