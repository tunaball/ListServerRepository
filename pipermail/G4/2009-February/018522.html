No acronyms and no freaking apostrophes in plural forms. Let's review  
the basics - we add an "s" to most noun phrases to mark them as  
plural and we add apostrophe-s ('s) to a noun phrase to denote  
ownership. One exception to this, however, is "its" and "it's."  
"It's" is a contraction of "it is," while "its" represents ownership.  
Because pronouns have separate forms for the plural (really just one:  
they), there will be no confusion of the meaning of "its."

So for the sake of my sanity, please stop copying the typographical/ 
orthographical errors of others. Rise above it!
