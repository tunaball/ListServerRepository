Here is a way to fire it up in Classic, but I'm not sure that Vuescan will
work in Classic.  Under OS9, software talks directly to the hardware.  Under
OSX, software talks to OSX which talks to the hardware.  Classic is a
process under OSX, so software is not permitted to talk directly to the
hardware.  That may make the OS9 app fail to work under Classic.  But try it
out and see.

To launch Vuescan in Classic, do this:

A) Hold down the Control key and click on the Vuescan icon.
B) Select "Show Package Contents"
C) Open the folder "Contents"
D) Open the folder "MacOSClassic"
E) Drag the "Vuescan" icon to the dock.  (I tried making an alias and that
wouldn't work.)
F) Click on the Vuescan icon in the dock.
