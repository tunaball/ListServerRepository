capable. I'd say yes.


>
> **Regarding the OSX "Classic"" mode  most of the
> applications I use for my business are running on OS
> 9.1 so would they run on the OSX Classic mode?

Classic is 9.2 (compatible w/ 9.1 apps) running inside OSX and will  
accomodate lots of software but even so,
IT is EASY to keep your OS9 bootable.
I am running OSX10.4.3 and can still boot to OS9.2.2 if I need to.



>
> **How does one got "get" from OSX to Classic  mode or
> do you have to re-boot the computer or can you toggle
> from one OS to the other?

Again, classic is different from booting to 9. You can have the  
ability to do either.
  Classic turns on and off as a preference.

>
> **Will all my periperals still work in OSX and  will I
> be able to use my Samsung ML-1210 printer with OSX?

Even if they don't you could still restart to 9 and they would.  
Meanwhile you get all the OSX goodies.

>
> **Should I consider buying a new dual boot computer
> with a faster processor or is my 400 mhz. enough?

New computers aren't dual boot and won't run 9 at all (just classic  
which may not be enough support for you).
If you simply UPGRADE your OS you will keep the 9-10 dual boot  
capability while gaining modern goodies.
