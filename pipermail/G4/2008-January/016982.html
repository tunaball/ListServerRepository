Or there's Neo Office, also free and does not need X11.

And lookint at macsales.com, that system can accept a dual CPU  
upgrade just like my own 1.6 GHz dual CPU upgrade. If you want that  
old Mac to become modern, I recommend that and a graphics card of at  
least Radeon 9600-9800 quality. I'm not sure what's available from  
nVidia, but they seem much less supportive of Mac than ATI.

With those upgrades and my RAM maxed out at 1.5 GB, this system plays  
World of Warcraft really well. It doesn't have the recommended DDR  
RAM, but still books. Naturally, more ordinary tasks like video  
encoding and what-not are a breeze. I also installed a Samsung DVD/CD  
burner that's been great. I had an LG drive before that that had  
problems with the drive sleep function in Tiger, so that's why I  
mention Samsung by name.
