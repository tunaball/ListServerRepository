I just finished reading the February, 2005 issue of MacWorld, and, in 
particular, the article "Prevent Mac Disasters."  It had a lot of 
good advice, and, again in particular, clearing out caches and logs 
to speed system performance.  So, I used Onyx to clear our most 
caches and logs, and re-started (Onyx makes you do just that), and 
WOW!  Safari is snappy once again.  In fact, everything seems much 
snappier.  Unbelievable, almost like a new computer.   The February 
issue is not online yet, but when it is, and if you do not subscribe 
to the printed version, I heartily recommend that article.
