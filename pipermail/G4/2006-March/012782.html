Originally I used a PC running Goldwave for importing the audio and  
separating the tracks, and a program called Wave Corrector for  
removing the clicks and pops.  I would record an entire LP (both  
sides) at 45 RPM and then use Goldwave to convert it back to the  
proper speed.  This method worked very well for the most part.  Then  
when I got my MDD dual 867, I also bought a complete stereo system  
off Ebay to go along with it.  Since I got it, I used Amadeus II to  
import the audio and separate the tracks.  I use ClickRepair to  
remove the clicks and pops.  I mostly have all my records done except  
for some obscure comedy albums.  I''m mostly working on my audio tape  
collection now.  As for which was better (PC or Mac), I'd have to say  
they were pretty equal, with each system having advantages over the  
other as far as software.  HTH
