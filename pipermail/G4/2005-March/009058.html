On 30 Mar, 2005, at 20:36, Jim Robertson wrote:

I imagine I can send the computer off to Apple, and they'll likely 
replace
the entire motherboard, or perhaps I can purchase a PCI card.

If you have the time to wait, you could always go to eBay, look up any 
PCI ethernet cards with Mac compatibility, bid on them, win one, send 
your payment, wait for the card to be delivered to you, install it, and 
try it out.

Or, you could avoid all that delay by contacting your nearest Mac 
dealer and buy a PCI 10/100/1000 ethernet card directly from them that 
would be guaranteed to be compatible. This way is quicker and you could 
be sure of getting a fast card that will work for you. It is also a 
whole lot cheaper than replacing the MoBo and you can do the 
installation yourself.
