Four hundred bucks for a dual 1 GHz seems like a pretty good deal to 
me, although I opted for the 1.4 GHz single myself.
"Fully symmetrical" is somewhat of a misnomer. Each application must be 
written to be "threaded" to take advantage of mulitprocessing. OS X is 
multi-threaded, so each processor will be involved, but I doubt you 
would ever see them with exactly the same load. By necessity, there 
must be a primary thread that coordinates all other activities, so one 
processor will typically have a greater load than the other. Other 
applications may or may not be threaded and therefore might or might 
not take advantage of multiprocessors. Many of the major apps, like 
Photoshop, etc., are threaded, but many older ones are not, and most OS 
9 apps are limited to one processor (there are a few notable 
exceptions).
