Mike,

Later versions of OSX and the G4 line seem to be far pickier about RAM than
other machines.  The RAM sticks specs 
need to be fully conforming in all respects to Apple's requirements.  IIRC
this has mainly to do with the internal 
speed of the chip and its having minimal variability in processing speed. 
Most quality sellers will refund or replace 
RAM that doesn't perform in your machine.

The easier solution would be that the RAM stick is not fully seated.  But
that sounds unlikely in your case.


Best of luck,
