Yes but if he want home theater then this usually implies multi-channel sound not just stereo. Nearly all pc's have that built in. There aren't very many Mac sound cards since sound has nearly always been a built in feature. My guess is that a firewire or usb connected sound module will be required to get 5.1 sound.

My "home theater" employs a G4 DP450MHz with a SATA card and big disk. It has a Logitech wireless mouse and keyboard. The stereo out goes to a small amp and speakers, while the VGA out goes to my Samsung flat panel. This allows me to watch "video files of dubious origin that my son gets from places on the web" :-) I use the free VLC s/w for viewing.

Aren't I naughty!
