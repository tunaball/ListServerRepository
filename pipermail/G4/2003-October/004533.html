On Thu, 23 Oct 2003 Tracker at aol.com wrote:

> Not all Macs play well with "Cable Select" settings even though many people 
> will say that they will IF the Mac is a newer machine. Older models, and the 
> occasional new one too, refuse to accept CS bridge settings and will not allow 
> the drive to mount until the setting is changed to Master or Slave.
> 
> Additionally, some Macs will refuse to recognize a drive set to CS if there 
> is a second drive also set to CS on the same bus ribbon. In that case, try 
> setting the drives to Master and Slave and install them on the IDE bus ribbon with 
> the Master drive closest to the mother board.
> 
> Another stumbling block is whether the drives are attached to separate IDE 
> bus ribbons and there is another device (CD, Zip, Imation, etc) on the ribbon 
> with the drive. Frequently, the speed of the drive is reduced because of the 
> slower device being attached to the ribbon. I always set up the drives on one 
> ribbon and the other devices on their own ribbon to avoid possible slow-downs.
>
