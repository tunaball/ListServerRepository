This behaviour is the same for both drives, you say? My first thought is
to boot single-user (hold command + s at startup) and run fsck -y until
there are no filesystem errors to report. Not sure about checking the
external drive that way - best to fsck the boot drive and then use disk
utility on that after reboot. If the behaviour continues.. Well, could be
hardware or could be a bad system, but just what's wrong with it I sure
don't know.
