Which means, in other words, if you are going to change the jumpers on  
the processor module then you may as well change the jumpers on the  
current module and achieve a higher speed.

For information regarding G4 Sawtooth Overclocking see;

http://power-mac-g4.com/g4clockup.html

450 MHz processor should take overclocking to a speed of about 500 MHz

> 450 divided by 100 = 4.5
>
> that means the 450 processor module multiples the bus speed by 4.5  
> to get its clock.
>

> which is 400MHz. A downgrade!
>
> You would need to change the jumpers on the 533 module to 5 or 5.5  
> times to get back the clock speed. This would require soldering.
