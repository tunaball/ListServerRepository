Bob, before from sending the disk in to a professional data recovery 
outfit such as DriverSavers.com, try ProSoft's Disk Rescue X, or 
their latest, Data Rescue II. Costs about a hundred bucks and you 
will need a drive with enough space to recover your data onto. See 
URL:
http://www.prosofteng.com/products/data_rescue.php

Bottom line is that you are going to lose some of your files (those 
that have been overwritten) but you will recover most of the disk. 
The price depends on how bad the need.

Good luck. I had the same problem once, but had done a backup a month 
before. So I just wiped the drive and told my wife some was wrong 
with Quicken and she would have to reenter all our banking data. She 
was not a happy camper!
