> Really, HIGHLY Not Recommended unless of course you know what you are

> light you up badly - even unplugged.  They store enough power to kill

IMHO, a wild exageration. I'd say it's highly unlikely the capacitor 
which provides the high voltage can store enough juice to kill a 
reasonably healthy adult. Does this mean you should pull the anode wire 
out and touch its tip with your tongue? Of course not. You should be 
very careful when messing about a CRT. A casual touch will produce an 
uncomfortable experience, definitely to be avoided, but it's not going 
to kill you or maim you. I'd say messing about with a power outlet is 
more dangerous in that respect.
