On Apr 25, 2005, at 3:51 AM, Johns Maillist wrote:

> The noted possibility of data loss in the case of one drive failing  
> is correct. But, I often wonder what happens if you do not run a  
> STRIPE set, just a standard drive set up, one ATA drive, and IT  
> fails. Do you not lose your data?

Yes, but the difference is that with RAID 0 you loose all of the data  
on both drives. With two individual drives you have the same chance  
of a hardware failure, but you only loose the data on the failed drive.


> The safer manner is to use mirrored system.

I agree. Drives are so inexpensive these days that it doesn't make  
sense to not use mirroring - and backups.
