On Sun, Apr 20, 2003 at 12:49:35PM -0400, Todd Flashner wrote:
> The only thing that bugs me, which is similar to what bugs me about Mail, is
> that you can't adjust how visited links look (in mail you can't adjust the
> typeface between read and unread messages). These are simple user interface
> issues which a strong program should allow some degree of control over
> (certainly on a Mac they should), and while small details, they make a big
> impact on readability and comfort to me.

Actually, you can. Write your own CSS (cascading style sheet) file describing
how you want links to look, and install it in the Advanced tab of the
preferences. Granted, that's not quite as easy as Really Large Preferences
Dialogs With Every Option You Ever Wanted, but it's not *that* hard either
(some 10 lines of text file).

Remember, Safari is meant to be simple and "good enough" for most users.
The CSS "escape" lets experts deal with most rendering issues without
cluttering up the preferences.
