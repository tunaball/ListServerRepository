ATA 100 drives work just fine with an ATA 66 controller (they act as ATA 66 
drives, which is no performance loss). The one gotcha is that ATA 66 
controllers can't deal with more than about 132GB (gigabytes) of disk, so a 
160GB (or larger) ATA 100 disk will not be able to use its full capacity.

Conversely, an ATA 33 drive could be used in your ATA 66 controller fine 
(at 33 speed). That you may even notice, if the drive is fast. :-)
