Scott,

I would chuck (get rid of) the USB drive.  Just write it off.  And, 
get an external Fire Wire drive, probably the Pioneer XX-105 (I 
forget the real letters for "XX").  You may also use it internally in 
place of the CD drive, and it should be recognized as a Super Drive 
(if your machine recognizes Super Drives).  I did just that (internal 
in my Quicksilver) and it works fine: burns CD's and DVD's.  Prices 
for the bare bones drive are about US$150 now, and you can get an 
external FW enclosure for about US$100.
