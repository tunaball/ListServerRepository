I'll just add one thing to Mels excellent explanation. Before you plug
in the cables, you'll probably have to remove the one screw directly in
front of the drive so you can easily lift it up in order to attach the
cables and attach the new drive to the tray.

Mel wrote:
> 
> jrs,
> I have the same machine, and adding the drive is painless. Screw the
> drive into the holder on top of the current drive, and connect the ATA
> and power cables, which are right there where they need to be. Make
> sure you set the new drive as Slave (assuming the current drive is
> Master) with the jumpers on the drive. If the jumpers are set
> incorrectly the computer will probably not boot (mine didn't), so don't
> panic if that happens. Just open it back up and check your settings.
> Configuration will vary, and the drive should include a diagram to show
> you how to do it. Apple has a PDF document that will help you here:
> http://www.info.apple.com/usen/cip/pdf/g4/atadrive.pdf
