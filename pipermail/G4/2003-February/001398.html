Superdrive will work without a problem in a Quicksilver.  You just have 
to exchange the CDRW for a SuperDrive.  My recommendation is to replace 
it with an actual Superdrive (the 104 drive, not the retail Pioneer AO4 
drive) because the 104 Superdrive is shorter in length and there is a 
guaranteed retrofit.

I now own a Dual-Mirror door 867Mhz G4, but prior to this I had the 
second generation 350Mhz AGP Sawtooth G4 (first generation being the 
PCI graphics 350).  Around 2 years ago, I removed the stock DVD-rom 
drive and replaced it with the new Pioneer AO3 DVD burner.  It fit 
without a problem, had plenty of space behind it to release heat, and 
Toast, iDVD, and DVD studio Pro all recognized it as a valid Superdrive 
(at that time model DVR-103).

When I got my new G4, I decided I would just exchange the stock CDRW in 
the 867 for the Pioneer A03.  I found that this wasn't an easy retrofit 
because the Pioneer was a longer drive and seemed to get to hot next to 
the vertical hard drives.  I went ahead and got a true Superdrive 
(DVR-104) from Mac Pros   and put it in the 867.  The Mac drive was a 
little thinner.  Not having a Quicksilver, I don't know how cramped 
things are in the box but it is something to consider.
