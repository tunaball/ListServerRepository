And the Imperial unit mile has to do with millia passuum, which I'll bet I have spelled wrong, but it's a thousand paces of a Roman Legion.

And the M is still a very common counting unit found in commercial purchase orders. A price per M, or $/M is widely understood to be dollars per thousand quantity. It is not a prefix in that usage.

Ant the capital letter K stands for a Kelvin which is a unit of temperature. People who care about precision use a small k for kilo. And they never use m for M. Little m is 1/1000.

And k is 1000 base ten. Using K or k for 2^10 or 1024 produces nothing but confusion but it's common in computer "science".
