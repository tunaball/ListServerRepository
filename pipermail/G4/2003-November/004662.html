A dual 1.25 G4 (according to benchmarks and comparisons I have seen) 
will perform about the same as a single 1.8 G5. The dual G4 will be 
faster if you are doing a lot of multitasking.  My dual g4 benchmarks 
at 162(with a fast hard drive) using x bench, while the dual G5's are 
posting numbers around 200, single 1.6 around 140, and single 1.8 
around 165.

You can get a lot more memory in a G5, but you only have half the space 
for internal expandability of hard drives and optical drives.

The G5's performance might increase, over time, as the OS and 
applications are optimized for the new processor and architecture.

I think the dual G4's are a better value as far as bang for the buck. 
you'll get mid range G5 performance for mid range imac price.
