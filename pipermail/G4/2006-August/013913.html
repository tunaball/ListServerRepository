On 28 August, 2006, at 6:45:04, Richard Ramsowr wrote:

> While were on the subject of Appleworks I noticed that
> I have no less than than 4 copies of it on my hard
> drive. How does one go about removing duplicate
> software from ones drive without effecting anything else.

The first thing you have to do is determine which version of the  
program you want to keep. Do this by getting information on the  
program and look for the version number to select the newest version  
from all the rest.

Then, simply select the one(s) you don't want any more and send them  
to the trash and empty it.

Almost forgot, check inside each application folder for any documents  
that you might want to keep on your drive and move them to the folder  
that you are going to keep. You shouldn't throw out your important  
documents, they may have some future value.
