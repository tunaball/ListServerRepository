>I've had this dual 866MHz G4 machine, currently running 10.2.6,
>since last November and it has froze up many times, and this is
>without running classic or doing anything particularly stressful to
>it.  It was more routine when I would leave the machine to go to
>sleep like they say you are supposed to let it, and is less frequent
>now that I power down when not using it.

That shouldn't be happening! OS 10 is generally very stable. Has it 
been doing this ever since you got it? If so, since the machine is (I 
assume) still under warranty, you might check with Apple (or the 
retailer you bought it from) as to returning it for replacement.


>   Whats more it has a funny
>(hilarious) habit of dialing forever but never connecting when I
>try to dial up my ISP.  I've taken it to the shop for this but they tried
>it many times and of course it dialed perfectly every time for them.
>So part of the routine for this machine is that I sometimes power it
>up, dial the ISP, can't connect, power it down, power it up, and then
>finally connect.

Weird! My first thought was that there's some sort of configuration 
problem. But since it works fine "at the shop" I'm wondering if 
there's ANYTHING (tiny as it might seem) you do differently at home 
vs in the shop.

Another thought - do you have lots of other stuff and/or long cords 
on that phone line? If so, try removing some of the other stuff and 
try to connect. It might be that the line doesn't have enough power 
to give a reliable connection. I've seen that happen!

Good luck!

Tom
