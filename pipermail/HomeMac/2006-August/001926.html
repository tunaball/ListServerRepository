I believe Thom Holland may have gotten you scared for no reason.  An 
ATA is sometimes known as an IDE.  I have installed an ATA-6 in a G3. 
The ATA/IDE standard is so common in the IBM world that backward 
compatability to ATA is almost a given. I can give you a published 
reference on ATA/IDE if you are interested.  Check the Apple 
Knowledge Base at http://search.info.apple.com/ (note the absence of 
"www")  for further information.  Apple can also supply manuals at 
http://www.info.apple.com/manuals  the last time I downloaded one.

Hope this gives you the confidence to forge ahead on updating your 
G4.  I am very much interested in your results because I too have a 
G4 and want to use it for my OS 9 mathematics files of the past ten 
years, as well as OS X.  Best of luck and keep in touch.
