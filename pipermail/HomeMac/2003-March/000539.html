There are HDs that put out a lot of heat and there are those that run 
cool to the touch. Putting my hand inside my G4 tower with 6 HDs of 
from different manufacturers says that Maxtor is hot, IBM is cooler, WD 
is almost cold, Seagates are hot and cold. So I guess it depends on the 
exact drive installed. My original iMac CPU the 333 was upgraded to 466 
with a daughter card. The upgrade ran hotter, but GaugePro said it was 
under 80 degrees even in the summer. I don't have air conditioning.

As far as making noise while not in use it sounds as though your 
computer is indexing either from Sherlock of Norton FileSaver.

You can run PeekaBoo ( shareware ) to see what is going on while it is 
" idle "
