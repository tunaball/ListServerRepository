Cat5 cables are designed for 100 Megbits/sec. If someone took the time  
to put the connectors on correctly and used a high quality cat5 cable,  
it could do 1000 Megbits/sec but no guarantee.

If you have some Cat5 cables lying around, you could try them and see  
what happens. It won't blow anything up. Worse case, the computers  
just won't communicate.

A cat6 cable is designed to do 1000 Megbits/sec or Gigabit. With a  
cat6 cable, you will have no problems.

It's your choice if you want to spend money on a high quality Cat6  
cable, or try cables sitting around for nothing.

You can use Activity Monitor (in the utilities folder) to check your  
network transfer rates.
