On 6  May2008, at 8:53 AM, Robert wrote:

> We picked up the adapter kit as the wife is going to iceland. In  
> the documentation they have picture of the adapters with countries  
> listed but Iceland is not there. Can someone tell me if it has the  
> adapter for Iceland and which one is it.


	Iceland seems to use two, round prongs, which I think are typical of  
continental Europe.  Current seems to be 220.  This should not be a  
problem for computers, since Macs tend to be dual voltage.

	BTW, I found a plug adapter at a US hardware store that adapts any  
plug to any socket.  It's made by Travel Smart, is about the size of  
four packs of gum, has a built-in surge suppressor, and cost  
somewhere around US$10.  It worked well for me in about a dozen  
countries in Latin America, where configurations changed from one  
country to the next.
