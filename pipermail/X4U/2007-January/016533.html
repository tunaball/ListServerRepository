You miss my point. Now almost any CD/Radio/iPod box is sold as HiFi. 
Do you regard a system with a 60Hz cutoff as HiFi?  I don't - but 
that's what it says on the box. 40 years ago a radio / record player 
with a spec that could be classed as HiFi today would be sold as a 
Radiogram. They'd be laughed at if they sold it as HiFi. (Modern 
high-end specs are much better though - compared to old High Street 
products; partly due to speaker cone materials). To do good bass you 
need to shift some air. Tiny speakers can't do that.

As to speaker cables, I meant cables used for speakers in modern kit 
with 'HiFi' on the box rather than proper cables made for speakers. I 
looked at systems in an electrical goods chain store recently and I'd 
rate the cable they used at no more than an amp. The twist tie 
bundling it up was thicker (well, almost).
