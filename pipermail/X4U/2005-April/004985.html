> How do you fit 2 hours of movie onto a DVD-R (+R or whatever)?

As long as its not over 2 hours, I'm OK. Either way, just rip with 
MacTheRipper, run the needed VOB files through MPEG Streamclip (convert 
to MPEG -just changes the wrapper, not the data), then burn with Toast 
(no menu). No transcoding necessary, meaning no loss in quality. If 
more than 2 hours just burn some of the files onto a 2nd DVD. Having to 
split into 2 DVDs is annoying, but no more so than the junk at the 
beginning.

Keep in mind this strips out all the extras, including menus. I should 
also mention you need to buy the MPEG2 Quicktime plugin from Apple to 
do the MPEG Streamclip step. In some cases you can skip that step or 
just burn the entire folder created by MacTheRipper and set it up to 
remove the UOPs.
