Personally, I think this is going about it the wrong way and is an  
inappropriate use of technology. You should lay down boundaries and  
educate your kids about the perils and pitfalls of the web, how you  
expect them to behave, yadda, yadda, and then (semi-) trust them to  
be responsible. Or do you lock your kids in their room, only letting  
them out at pre-defined periods? (That question sounds more  
aggressive than I mean it to be. It's an analogy. Please don't take  
it personally).

OK, (semi-) trust them because you will be monitoring them. Let them  
know that you log their activity. If need be, tell them that you've  
got some remote access software on their machines and that you'll pop  
in to check on them occasionally (and make sure you do and you do if  
you say you will!). Let them grow and take responsibility for their  
actions under your watchful (not controlling) eye.

Of course, your kids, your choice. I just worry about humankind's  
natural instinct to monitor and control and where it'll take us.
