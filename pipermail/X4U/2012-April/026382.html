Neil,
I agree, assuming you can get the needed capacity and performance with standard FW drives.

My only suggestion was, if the NAS is used as a file server, Joe should still have backups to a separate device. For instance, the ReadyNAS supports rsynch over the LAN or across the Internet, so you can back up your NAS to another, remotely located NAS if you like. Depending upon the NAS model, it also suports rsynch over SSH so your data is encrypted as it transits the network.
