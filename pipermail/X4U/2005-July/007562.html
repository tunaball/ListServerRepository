I am feeling feisty today so... Comparing a c/c++ program to something 
like FileMaker isn't a fair comparison. C vs Pascal works; FileMaker vs 
4th Dimension works too. While your remark is enlightening, it is a bit 
like comparing the speed test of a quark and that of a minivan.

Given the very high level of FileMaker "zero programming" scripting, I 
suggest a better test would involve activities typical of the business 
application of the software. Say, create 20,000 invoice records, modify 
each of them, then delete them all one by one. Run this on the target 
systems and see how you do.
