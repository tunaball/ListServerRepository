>> I'm considering migrating from Mail.app to an open source app,

>
> I'm interested to know what's wrong with Mail.app for your purposes.

There's something I don't like in Mail.app that made me consider  
switching (I didn't finally): it is the fact that when editing quoted  
text in replies (inserting blank lines, deleting some parts, etc.)  
blank lines between quoted text and new text as seen in the editor do  
not necessarily coincide with blank lines in the raw source. In  
consequence they are not necessarily what other readers of your mail  
see, in particular some blank lines disappear between quoted text and  
new text.

If formatting of plain text mail is important for you, as in my case,  
that's worrying, you can't trust the editor.

The real solution would be to fix that. A partial solution/feature  
would be to allow the user to turn fancy quote bars off and see the  
actual text and quote marks in the editor to know what he is editing  
for certain, but AFAIK that's not possible by now.
