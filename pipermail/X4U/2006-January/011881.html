Yes. The 5k limit was introduced early-to-mid 2005, I think, but I  
have plenty of insightful & worthwhile messages here in my archive  
that were posted before then & which are longer.

A size limit is a great idea for filtering messages which contain  
entire digests full of quoting, but fails to accommodate slightly  
longer posts (eg 6k or 8k) that are genuinely useful & interesting.  
As you'll have noticed the message you receive when your posting was  
rejected states that it'll be reviewed by a moderator - I'd certainly  
not encourage you to break or circumnavigate the rules if this were  
the case, but larger messages never reach the list so I can only  
assume they never get reviewed.

Since breaking the size limit is an unusual circumstance and no-one's  
ever complained about two-part reposts before I don't think it's a  
problem. Undoubtedly if there's disagreement then we'll have  
discussion on the subject and consensus will eventually be reached.
