I have just upgraded from iPhoto '05 to iPhoto '06. Prior to this I 
duplicated the working iPhoto '05 "iPhoto Library" so I have a viable 
backup / back out plan. Launching iPhoto '06 for the first time asks 
about updating the iPhoto Library. I accept this but iPhoto 
immediately tells me that the iPhoto Library is "damaged and can not 
be upgraded" with quit being the only option.

After this I followed the following sequence:

1. Duplicated original Ver 5 (5.0.4) iPhoto Library
2. Ran "iPhoto Library Manager" (IPLM) on duplicate and imported to a 
new (3rd) library.
3. Recorded the errors.
4. Deleted damaged files for duplicate.
5. Made a duplicate of the "cleaned up" library (as a back out).
6. Set iPhoto '05 to read the cleaned up library instead of the original.
7. Launched iPhoto '05 - unexpectedly quit.
8. Went back to duplicate of cleaned up library (from step 5).
9. Repeated step 2.
10. No errors reported.
11. Restored iPhoto '05 (Ver 5.0.4).
12. iPhoto crashed when attempting to open cleaned library.
13. Deleted com.apple.iPhoto.plist
14. Opened the Terminal and typed chflags -R nouchg 
~/Pictures/iPhoto\ Library/ into the window followed by a Return.
15. Confirmed access rights.
16. Attempted to launch iPhoto '05.
17. iPhoto '05 crashed.
