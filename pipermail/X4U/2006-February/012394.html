I have both -- I know *many* graphics professionals who have both. I can't
always be home, so the PowerBook allows me to work away from home. A
PowerBook allows me to make presentations in clients offices; it allows me
to work for short spells in client offices, it allows me to work on
vacation, er, I mean work from my "remote office" in Florida or Texas or
France.

It's certainly not as quick nor comfortable to work on my 1GHz 17" PowerBook
with 1GB RAM as it is to work on my dual 2GHz G5 with 2.5GB RAM and a dual
monitor setup (23" Cinema Display and 17" Studio Display LCD). But, it's an
acceptable substitute when I simply am not able -- or choose not to -- work
at home.
