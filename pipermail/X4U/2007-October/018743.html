On 10/28/07 1:53 PM, Rod Duncan wrote:

> I agree. However, I find it disconcerting that Apple it OVERCHARGING
> Canadian users for the same service. By over 40%!

There are additional costs associated with doing business outside the
country of origin. What value the Canadian dollar has against the U.S.
dollar is but one *part* of the pricing equation -- and even if that was the
whole of it, it changes so frequently that it would be impossible to
continually update packaging, etc., to reflect the current exchange rate.
Besides the exchange rate on the day of sale, there are the continued costs
of service during the year -- a year during which the exchange rate
fluctuates even more.

What you call overcharging may in fact not be.
