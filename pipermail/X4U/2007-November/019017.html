It just depends whether it's worth the hassle for them to do so.

It's obviously enough of a chore as it is to install a Hackintosh -  
the research required to work out how to do it in the first place  
must be quite something. But hackers will (likely) eventually get  
around any restrictions the Apple were to make - the open nature of  
the BSD codebase facilitates this - and they probably represent such  
a small amount of lost revenue that it's not worth Apple's while to  
pay a programmer to make it harder.
