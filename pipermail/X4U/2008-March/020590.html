Putting a folder at the root of the external and adding additional  
data there won't have any effect on the bootability of the the  
external drive. Depending on how you're doing the mirroring  
though . . . you'll need to make sure that your software (SuperDuper,  
PSyncX, Disk Utility, CarbonCopyCloner, whatever) is set to not delete  
files from the destination . . . otherwise since the extra folder at  
the root of the external isn't on the internal it will get deleted.
