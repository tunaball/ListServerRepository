On 12/23/06 6:46 PM, karen spitzer wrote:

> I've upgraded to a MacBook and want to sell my TiBook - 512k ram. Would you
> suggest parting it out, and if so where do I research prices.

I just sold my old G4 Quicksilver on eBay. I started by searching for that
model, and similar, in the Completed Listings, to see what they had been
selling for; took a look at what the Buy It Now prices were on currently
available auctions, and then added in what I thought the extras I had were
worth (second hard disk, maxed out RAM, internal Zip, extra video card,
etc.). 

I also took a look at some online used vendors to see what they sell similar
Macs for. Try Google for Used Mac Prices and poke around the sites that are
returned. For example, right now Powermax.com has a 550mHz TiBook (50mHz
faster than yours) for $599 -- they are certified used and provide a
warranty, which you can't do/offer (probably!), but it's a place to start
deciding a price. headgap.com has a G4/500 TiBook for $599.77, including
shipping.

I spent a year posting on Mac groups, Craig's List, etc., with no bites.
Sold the dual 800 quicksilver in four days on eBay with a Buy It Now price I
was satisfied with, plus buyer paid shipping.
