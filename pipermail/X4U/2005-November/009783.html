Just a couple other possibilities for people. First, if you have an  
Apple Store nearby, you can usually get the upgrades there (bring in  
a blank CD-R). Universities and Community Colleges often have places  
to get downloads like this too. And finally, don't forget your local  
Macintosh User Group (MUG). They're usually well-equipped to handle  
such things. In fact, if you bring your Mac to a meeting, they may  
even do it for you while you kick back and eat munchies! See <http:// 
www.apple.com/usergroups/find/> to find a group near you. And no, you  
don't have to be a fanatic to get help from a MUG.
