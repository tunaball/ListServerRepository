   What sets BitTorrent apart is its very robust technique for rewarding
   specifically the peers which upload the most, known as leech
   resistance. On the highest level, this prevents a long-term meltdown
   of the system from being caused by people running leeching clients.
   It also causes upload and download rates to be somewhat correlated,
   so peers on good pipes get decent download rates, which increases
   general good feeling about how the system behaves.

   Bram Cohen, http://interviews.slashdot.org/interviews/ 
03/06/02/1216202.shtml?tid=126&tid=185&tid=95

I haven't checked lately - it's been about 3 years since I really  
used BitTorrent - but my experience has been that if you don't share  
you get near-zero download speeds. Note that many clients allow you  
to limit the upload rate of your client - this prevents you  
saturating your broadband connection - but setting the limit to "0"  
generally means "no limit" rather than 0 kbps.

It's also considered quite rude to leech BitTorrent, but I appreciate  
that if one's kid is pirating music or software or films then he may  
(in some jurisdictions?) only be prosecuted for his uploading, not  
his downloading.
