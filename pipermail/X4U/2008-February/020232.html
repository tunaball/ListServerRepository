I run Parallels' virtual HD from an external Firewire 800 2.5-inch  
drive, which saves a bunch of space for me on my MacBook Pro.
Here are some basic instructions, if you want to give it a go . . .

Most likely your virtual machine is located in Documents - Parallels -
Microsoft Windows XP. Copy this folder to your external HDD. (Note that
I said copy, not move.)

Enter the virtual machine's folder. Press and hold down the Apple key on
your keyboard and run the .PVS file. When you see the summary screen,
release the Apple key. Click on "Hard Disk 1" Correct the path to the
.hdd file, so that you will open the file on your external hdd, not on
your Mac.

Click OK, and try running your virtual machine. It should run OK now.

A word of caution: if your external hard drive is formatted into FAT32,
files bigger than 4GB cannot fit on it. So, if your virtual machine is
bigger than that (or grows big enough in the process), the whole process
may not work.

It took a bit of trial and error, but all's well now. This setup runs  
as fast as when the HD was on the laptop's internal drive.
