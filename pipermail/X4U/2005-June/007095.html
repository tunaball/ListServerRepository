On 6/16/05, Samantha Cornell <samantha at netresults.biz> wrote:
> A friend is attempting to install the Macromedia Suite on a G5 iMac
> running Tiger and is having problems.  He is installing using a
> burned copy of the CDs (he is a teacher and is installing it on a
> school computer that is summering at his home...the tech department
> does have the licenses...but they will not release the originals)
> 
> It is a 4 disc installation.  He gets through the first disc fine, it
> ejects, asks for disc 2.  When he puts it in, it flashes on the
> screen, then immediately closes and the install stops.  Outside of
> the install process, he can open the contents of the CD, browse, and
> it is fine.
> 
> Any tips?
> 

He has the license code, correct? If so, he (or someone with a
broadband connection) should download the files from Macromedia's
website. Assuming you're talking about the latest version (MX 2004).
If he has dial-up, anyone can burn those downloaded files to CD/DVD
for him. They are 30 day full-use demos. When he starts the first
application, it will ask if he wants to demo the software or if he has
a serial number. He should click the serial number radio button and
enter the code.
