Hi all,

iMac 400 Mhz DV, OS 10.3.9. Safari or IE.

Ever since I've upgraded from 10.3.8 to 10.3.9 I've been having 
problems browsing the internet at home. Every time I click on a link my 
modem resets. I have a DHCP broadband connection through my local cable 
provider. The modem is an Ericson brand, I'm not at home to get the 
actual model number from it. Whenever I click on a link, if I watch my 
modem, all the lights turn off and then it goes through it's start up 
cycle. Once the modem has reset itself I cannot connect to the internet 
until I go to System Preferences > Network > Built in Ethernet and 
click the "Renew DHCP lease" button. After doing this the link in my 
web browser finishes loading. As soon as I click on a new link the 
whole process repeats itself. This is very annoying as it takes about a 
minute or more for every link I click on. I've tried Safari, IE, and 
Netscape with the same results so I know it's not the browser itself. A 
service tech will come by to check the modem but not until Saturday 
evening.  Email and iChat and certain games and such that require an 
internet connection work fine, It's only the web browsers that cause 
the problem. Has anyone heard of this problem before?
