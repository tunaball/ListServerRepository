Kansas, you need to hold down command-s to enter single user mode.   
It might give you a hint as to why the update caused the problem, or  
it might not.  You can try fscking the drive in single user mode, you  
can try to repair permissions via OS X install CD.  I tried these and  
there was no change.  I figured it would take more time to figure out  
the exact cause and try to fix it than to just archive and  
reinstall.   35 minutes later I had a working 10.4 install, then  
updates to 10.4.1, etc. , worked fine.  1 other of the 30-odd  
computers I support had the same problem. (Yes, we repaired  
permissions prior to the update).

Sorry.  Maybe someone here can troubleshoot you back to a working  
system, but it's my bet that you'll save time by just archiving and  
reinstalling.
