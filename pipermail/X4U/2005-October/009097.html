Exactly the point Philip.

Applecare is insurance, pure and simple. The cardinal rule of 
insurance should be "don't buy it if you can afford the consequence 
of not having it". Most people can afford to replace their 
PowerBook/hard drive/mac mini/ whatever. Insure your house, maybe. 
Your car, maybe. Your life, probably. But your computer? That makes 
no rational financial sense.

In every case I am aware of, an "extended warrantee" is one of a 
manufacturer's or retailer's major profit centers. I seriously doubt 
it is any different at Apple.

Buy Applecare, sleep well and boost Apple's stock. That's fine, but 
don't believe that on average over the long haul you will be saving 
yourself money.

My 2 cents
