On Oct 31, 2005, at 5:46 AM, Jim Robertson wrote:

> I know that the ATA controller in the Sawtooth (my son has the 450 MHz
> version) supports only 128 GBytes per drive. What if I put in a  
> larger drive
> but partition it? Is the limitation 128 MBytes per volume or for the
> physical device?

The limitation is "for the physical device," as described in the  
following Apple Support document:
<http://docs.info.apple.com/article.html?artnum=86178>

However, I have two >128 GB drives in a Server G4 (Sawtooth), both of  
which are used to full capacity through the ATA Hi-Cap Support Driver  
in Intech SpeedTools, which is available at a good price from OWC:
<http://eshop.macsales.com/item/Intech%20USA/SPEEDHCCD/>
