The built-in speech capabilities of OS X will not do transcription,  
but iListen will - www.macspeech.com

However, it requires "training" the system to recognize your voice -  
so I'm not sure how good it would be for interviews where there are  
voices that have not been used for training. Not to mention the fact  
that typically, recorded interviews by grad students are not done  
with high quality recording equipment, so the voices aren't always  
very clear (I know, I'm finishing up my own dissertation).

What I ended up doing was recording all of my interviews on an  
Olympus digital recorder and then emailing the DSS audio files to a  
transcriptionist who then emailed back the interviews in Word format  
a few days later.
