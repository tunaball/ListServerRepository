My experience regarding hard drives is that quality control in the  
last few years may be going downhill.  We had 20 beige G3's (ca.  
1998) and none of their HDs have failed, these are 6-8 GB drives.  As  
we've upgraded to newer machines, I've put these drives into new G4  
towers (it makes the user feel better to still see all their stuff,  
and gives them an emergency boot disc after I upgrade the system  
software).  I've had 3 HDs out of 20 fail in the last 4 years, but  
all of them were the drives that shipped with the new towers.  The  
older drives keep chugging along.  Is it because corruption is more  
likely as drive density increases?
