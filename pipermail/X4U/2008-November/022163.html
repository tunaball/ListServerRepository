Unix ownerships, groups, and ACLs (Access Control Lists that extend 
the basic Unix model) are always specified within the system by their 
numeric value. "unknown" just means that the ownership/permissions 
are given to users (numbers) who have no corresponding name. That 
also means that it's very difficult for a process to run as that user 
in order to take advantage of those permissions.

Nonetheless the entries should not be there. Was the Mac in stock at 
a shop when you got it? Could it have been a demo model? Is there 
anything unusual about the history of the disc drive?
