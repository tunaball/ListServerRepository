In a similar situation I use wireless. The reason is that I have two 
offices and both have wireless that I set up with common passwords 
and such; thus when I turn the computer on I am connected no matter 
where I am. The computer is not even aware that I changed locations. 
Ethernet would require surgery on the building in the other office.

My son uses wireless at college and here. He just turns on his 
PowerBook and is up and running. There is a computer in our home that 
can't do wireless, so the router is connected to a switch in the 
basement that connects to that computer and to a sewing machine and 
to ....
