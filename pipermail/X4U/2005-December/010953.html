Services are implemented "free" as part of any Cocoa application.  
Services only work in Carbon applications like the Office suite if  
the programmers specifically implement the functionality. An example  
of a Carbon app that supports services is TextWrangler (and its big  
brother BBEdit). So you are correct that Microsoft is responsible for  
services not working in Office and IE.
