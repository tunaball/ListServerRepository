On Jan 19, 2005, at 12:28 PM, Eddie Hargreaves wrote:

> Syncing bookmarks via .Mac does exactly what its words describe. It
> synchronizes your bookmarks for every computer that you choose to sync.
> Every computer you use would have the exact same bookmarks (in Safari 
> only)
> Since your bookmarks bar is part of your bookmarks, that is included.
>
> How it works is that you first choose one computer to sync, and the
> bookmarks from that machine are uploaded to the .Mac server space. 
> When you
> set another machine to sync, it will download the bookmarks from .Mac 
> and
> overwrite the existing file. Any changes you then make from the second
> machine are uploaded to .Mac and then downloaded from .Mac to the first
> machine, etc.
>
> This is also true for the address book. You requested that your work
> addresses be copied to your home computer but not vice versa. That is 
> not
> syncing, that is copying. You can do that manually. Maybe an 
> AppleScript
> could be written to make it a scheduled process. Maybe one is already
> available.
>
> Eddie Hargreaves
