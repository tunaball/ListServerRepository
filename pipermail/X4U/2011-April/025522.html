Cheryl, don't use the nist.gov machines.  They are Stratum 1 clock
sources; they get their times from atomic clocks and such (which are
considered Stratum 0 clock sources), and serves timing requests to
Stratum 2 clock sources.  Finally, Stratum 3 clock sources get their
times from Stratum 2 clock sources, and serves timing requests to the
general public (i.e. you and me).  NTP keeps clock information accurate
to within 10 ms.  If your clocks are off by 30 seconds, something is
broke and wrong.

I agree with Tommy; set all machines to sync to the same time servers.
