On 4 Feb 2006, at 11:59, Daly Jessup wrote:
> ....I forwarded port 80 on my Airport to my local IP address on my  
> Mac. The dav folder comes up perfectly here on my mac, using http:// 
> localhost.dav
>
> But at work, if I put "http://[myhomeipaddress]/dav" into a  
> browser, it fails to reach the dav folder saying something about  
> the client could not connect to the remote folder.

Check that your port-forwarding is working. From home visit http:// 
grc.com & see what ShieldsUp! reports of port 80, or from work  
`telnet myhomeipaddress 80`.

I don't think you should be connecting via a browser - I think that  
browsers are generally for reading the Internet, and not designed for  
upload. I take it you're using a Windows PC at work? I think that  
instead you should use one of the options in Network Neighbourhood or  
"My Network Places" ("add a new network place" in Windows XP?).

A *very* quick Google reveals: http://www.hss.caltech.edu/help/web/ 
webdav/accessing/windowsxp
