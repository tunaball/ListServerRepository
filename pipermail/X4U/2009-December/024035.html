On Dec 5, 2009, at 9:54 PM, John Baltutis wrote:

> On 12/5/09, Crandon David <tabdave at ca.rr.com> wrote:
>> 
>> Safari will autofill the URL you are typing in for a webpage if
>> you've visited it before. How do you remove a single "memory" of one
>> page from that list?
> 
> Safari->Preferences->Autofill and go through each list and delete the
> individual ones you don't wish to retain.

That works for save form values.
For URLs, click the bookmark icon in the bookmarks bar. Here you can organize bookmarks, but more importantly, remove individual history items. 

 Then type in the bookmark search field (below the google search field) enough to match the URL, then delete the ones you want gone.
