Paul,

Along with a power supply problem, there's a fairly rare condition  
(these days) that might be contributing to the problem restarts. As a  
hard disk ages, the lubricants in it can break down causing "stick- 
tion" -- this an inability to get the platters spinning up from a  
stopped condition with the lubricant acting as a break instead.  
Hitting it several times in a row may give enough momentum and/or heat  
up the lubricant that the drive starts working. The usual fix for  
"sticktion" is a new hard drive.
