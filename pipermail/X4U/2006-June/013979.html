Of course spoken English uses intonation, emphasis _and_ context in place of visual cues in printing...
In the example above, "I ate The Burger." would seem to mean that you ate somebody named "The Burger". Presumably, that would be a nickname and presumably hwoever you spoke to would know this and also know that somebody ate this guy...
But yes, you are right: The two sentences _would_ sound the same and, if spoken to the wrong person at the wrong time, _would_ be misunderstood.
Which is a very good case for using capitalization when writing! ;-)
