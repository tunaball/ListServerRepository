I just had a thought, it would be interesting to try the 2Ghz Mini with a
10,000RPM SATA drive in a FireWire case.  The only downside I can see is
that you'd be limited to FW400, rather than FW800.  Well, that and you'd
want to build the drive enclosure yourself with plenty of cooling most
likely.  I don't know how hot 10k SATA drives get, but a lot of the 10K SCSI
ones get plenty hot.

The whole external drive bit is the reason the only problem I see with the
Mini is the RAM.  I wonder how well a fully decked out Mini would handle
Aperture or Lightroom.  I know Aperture expects a pretty serious graphics
card.
