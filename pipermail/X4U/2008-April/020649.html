Coming from a G4/466 . . . the mini is a much better performer.

Compared to an iMac; the mini is a bit anemic; but not so much as you  
might think.

For your purposes . . . I would think it would be fine; the biggest  
issue I would have is that I consider 2 GB of RAM the bare minimum for  
Leopard and more is better. IIRC. I don't know the mini's RAM limits  
off hand . . .but that would be my biggest concern. If you can get at  
least 2 GB in it then I think you will certainly be satisfied with  
it . . . and given that you've already got the monitor/keyboard/mouse/ 
etc . . . it's hard to argue with the price.

I use a first generation mini as my file server and Time Machine  
server for a couple of laptops at home . . . and it's more than  
sufficient for that. Sitting down and logging into the local  
console . . . it's certainly far more apparently responsive than the  
G4/500 with Tiger that it replaced as a file server.

Go for it I would say, you won't be disappointed.
