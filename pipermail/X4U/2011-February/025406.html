At 5:51 PM -0500, 2/28/11, Michael Burton wrote:

>Here's a quick-n-dirty Applescript to find PowerPC-only applications.


Why suggest a quick-n-dirty AppleScript that 
won't look everywhere, and that some may question 
what it might 'do' to their computer. 
(AppleScripts can 'do' things and if an 
AppleScript 'does' something that we really 
didn't want it to do, we have a reason to be 
upset)

Rather use the always ready, always available, 
'About The Mac' in the upper left corner of every 
window. Then press the More Info... and select 
the Applications item within the Software group. 
Everyone will easily be able to trust that this 
method won't 'do' anything but tell you the 
names, version, and kind of applications on their 
drive. And it looks everywhere!
