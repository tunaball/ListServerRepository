Previous experience has shown it to be a better test of a new hard drive
than a simple erase.  By writing a zero to every location on the HD, you
know that at least initially you're able to do it.

I've seen a hard drive that I was able to erase, but about 80% in was bad
and it wouldn't handle a erase when writing zeroes to every location.

Of course on a 750GB drive takes half of forever, but not as long as doing
only one of the 3 recommended passes on a 2GB Seagate Barracuda SCSI HD when
installing RSTS/E on a PDP-11/73 (that ran for over 12).
