Besides focusing on the quality of the man behind the camera, also check out
more technical factors to image quality than just resolution.

Fuji's 5th generation of "Super CCDs" for instance is much better in terms
of light sensitivity than others.

>From the tests I have seen the F10 responds to low-light conditions with
much less visual noise: You can set it to 1600 sensitivity and get the
results that comparable cameras deliver at 400. Most other digital cameras
are practically useless if you set their sensitivity to 1600 although that
might well be an option in their menus.
