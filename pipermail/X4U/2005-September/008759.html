A spare hard drive is by far the best way to backup because you can  
schedule a backup on a regular and steady basis. If you leave it to  
manually backing up whenever you think you need to, there'll come a  
time when something unexpected happens and you've waited too long  
between manual backups.
I have been very pleased with two external firewire Mercury Elite  
drives purchased at OWC. Not the cheapest available, but one of the  
things they come bundled with is the OEM version of Intech's  
SpeedTools Utilities. of which QuickBack is an excellent, IMHO,  
backup utility which can be easily scheduled to produce bootable,  
full or incremental backups.
There are freewares and sharewares that also provide scheduled  
backups, but because I have QuickBack, I prefer using it.
