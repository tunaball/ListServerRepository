> One day I sat down and compared different bit rates to the original  
> CD, I took some works from my favourite black musician Erykah Badu.  
> All of them sound pourish, shades are lost in different degrees, so  
> you get to take a decission having a 20GB iPod. The chosen  
> compromise was finally 224 AAC, and 320 AAC for a few exceptions

Did you do a blind listening test? If not, this has no value. If you  
know what you're listening to, then you'll hear things that may not  
be there. Also, one musician is not enough.

I've done blind tests, and I've listened to music through excellent  
speakers and headphones. I've done this with classical and jazz,  
where recording quality is excellent, and where the music really  
needs good quality. I've compared AIFF, lossless and AAC, and find  
that AAC at 160 has a really good sound. I can tell the difference  
between AIFF and AAC at 160, for some music, but that difference is  
slight.
