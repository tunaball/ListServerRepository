I checked the developer's site (http://www.sente.ch/software/GPGMail/ 
English.lproj/GPGMail.html) and noted the following:

GPGMail & 10.4.1
MacOS X 10.4.1 will automatically disable all Mail bundles when  
encountering them the first time. To re-enable GPGMail (which works  
fine with 10.4.1), you need to:

     * Quit Mail
     * In Finder, rename folder $HOME/Library/Mail/Bundles (Disabled)  
back to $HOME/Library/Mail/Bundles.
     * In Terminal, type:

defaults write com.apple.mail EnableBundles 1
defaults write com.apple.mail BundleCompatibilityVersion 2

     * Relaunch Mail


Everything is fine now.
