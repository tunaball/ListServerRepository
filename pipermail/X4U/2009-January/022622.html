> Ed,
> This is covered nicely in Pages Help, which would be faster than  
> waiting for the list to answer.
>
> But anyway, click in any data cell in your table. Open the  
> Inspector, go to the Table tab.
> In the menu next to "Edit Rows and Columns," choose "Sort  
> ascending" or "Sort Descending," as you prefer.
>
> Another thing you can do, if you can make it work (which I can't)  
> is to make your table in Numbers and save the Numbers file. Then  
> select the table in Numbers and paste it into your Pages document  
> and save the Pages document. Then when you change the table in  
> Numbers (and save) supposedly you can then select the pasted table  
> in Pages, and a "Refresh" button is supposed to come up, which will  
> update the table in Pages.
>
> (Does anyone get that "Refresh" button when selecting a linked  
> table in Pages?)
>
> Or, you could make the whole document in Numbers, putting your text  
> into text boxes, and keeping your meds data in a Numbers table,  
> where you can do all kinds of spreadsheet-type operations on it.
>
> But if all you need is a simple sort, it's right there in Pages, in  
> the Table inspector.
