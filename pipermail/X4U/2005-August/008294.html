> Pretty easy, but you'll need to use Terminal.
> 
> Let's say the kid's short user name is "KID" and the second volume is
> named "VOL2".  You'll need to bring up Terminal and do:
> 
> sudo csh  (or any other shell)
> Password: <enter admin password>
> cd /Volumes/VOL2 (sets your working dir to the 2nd volume)
> cp -pr /Users/KID . (copies all of the kid's files...may take a while)
> 
> Now, take a minute with the finder to verify that the kid's files are
> on the second volume.  If satisfied, continue by doing:
> 
> cd /Users  (sets your working directory)
> mv KID KID-save  (rename the stuff for a safety backup)
> ln -s /Volumes/VOL2/KID (set up a symbolic link)
> 
> Now, log out and log in as KID.  Everything should be fine.  When
> you're really satsified, move the:
> 
> /Users/KID-save
> 
> safety backup to the trash.   You're done.
> 
> For the second kid, repeat with the short user name of the second kid.
