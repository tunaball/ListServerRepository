I have found Skype to be useful for talking with Windows users, Skype 
to Skype. iChat does the trick for the MacOS folks, of course. We have 
found the sound quality to be excellent except when there is another 
application using the internet bandwidth for a machine. Ditto for the 
Skype to the plain old telephone system.

One client declined to use it for fear of malicious activity such as 
harvesting sensitive data. I haven't detected any such problems yet but 
as I consider the client to be technically savvy I am being more 
vigilant. Recently, Skype bundled its software with Kazaa. Many people 
are concerned about this and not without good cause. Kazaa is a known 
and unrepentant promoter of spy-ware.

We are investigating other voip solutions but continue to use Skype 
until we satisfy ourselves that it is safe or not.
