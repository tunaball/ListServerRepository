It's an Adobe Postcript printer, so you shouldn't need any drivers.  
The most you would need is a PPD, which I would expect comes on the 
install disk.  The Dell Site says OS X is supported.

Note the 3100 adds Postscript to the 3000 for $100.  The price you 
quote makes me think you are looking at the 3000.  If you were really 
trying to save money, you could get the 3000 and rely on 
CUPS+GIMP-Print to drive it.  GIMP-Print uses ghostscript to render 
postscript and knows how to speak PCL to "Windows" printers.  See 
http://gimp-print.sourceforge.net/p_FAQ_OS_X.php3 for more info.  I 
have used GIMP-Print very successfully to talk to any number of random 
printers, but I have not used it specifically with a 3000.
