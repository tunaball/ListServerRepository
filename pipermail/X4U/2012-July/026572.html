Yes, well, back to Cat's experiences with Lion. What isn't clear is how many of the problems she reported were fixed in the later versions of Lion, and how many persist.

I can speak from my own firsthand experience, and that of my friend. We both stayed with SL until recently, and immediately moved to 10.7.4. My friend installed over top of SL, and I did a "clean install" on a new drive, but had the Migration Assistant copy applications, settings, etc. from my SL clone drive. For both of us, Lion has been generally trouble-free.

I really like Mission Control, and find that I customized it to my liking and use it all the time. This is in contrast with Spaces in SL, which I found a bit clunky for me. That said, I've seen some of the odd behavior that Cat mentioned, where applications sometimes appear in the "wrong" space. It's infrequent and, for me anyway, not a major irritant. But it's clearly a bug in Lion.

Neither of us has seen the other issues Can reports, but that doesn't make them any less real. As others have said, Snow Leopard was in many ways a significant bug-fix of Leopard. Maybe we'll see a similar relationship between Lion and Mountain Lion. We'll know soon enough!  :-)

Enjoy,

Jim

Let's say it's a document which you then see the Quickview of...sometimes that simply never goes away, even after you mouse away, and the Spotlight dropdown menu vanishes...that quickview preview just hangs there and only logging out and back in resolves it.
>

Mission control loses/breaks the relationships between app and space to which it is assigned. This happened rarely, if at all, in Snow Leopard.  Launchpad, you can set up how you like, but when you restart your Mac, your Launchpad arrangement is all re-arranged.
> 
> in *my* opinion, both of these features could be extremely useful, but they are so hosed that I stopped using them.
> 
> Perhaps the most serious problem I've had with Lion is with the mail app. I love OS X Mail. But Lion's Mail.app got my mailcount wrong. I use IMAP. and tho Lion mail "knew" how many emails there should be on the server, it would only display some of them.
> 
> Contrast this to an install of Snow Leopard on a different partition, Tiger on a G4 Mini and Panther on a Pismo, all of which got the mail count right. Thunderbird on Snow, Tiger and Lion got the mail count right. Lion Mail.app simply couldn't.
> 
> You might say that Mail is not OS X and I'll not get balled up in that debate. Mail.app comes with OS X and is ostensibly well-integrated with the OS in a variety of ways.

I have gone to the ends of the earth with apple tech support and others to try to solve them and there simply were no solutions to the problems I've listed. > 
> I must have clean-installed Lion on my Mac Pro at least 5 separate times. So, I feel I've been there and back with Lion issues.
> 
> I've read scores of comments from people who say "I don't have that problem," as though this means that I should not have it either. I would be tickled insane not to have had these problems. Yet, here I am.
