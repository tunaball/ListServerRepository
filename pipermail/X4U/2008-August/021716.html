First thing I'd check is how much free space you have left on your  
startup volume.  Low disk space=trouble, since virtual memory, log  
files, etc can take up that space, and make the computer churn the  
hard drive constantly.

Is there any improvement after restarting the machine?

Try making a "dummy" account, and logging into that account.  Does the  
problem persist? If so, might be some faulty preference files or  
system addons.

Have you installed any software that required you to enter your  
administrator password?
