I have had a MacSense MIH 130A router for five years or so and it has 
been one of the most reliable electronic instruments I have ever owned. 
It just never gives problems, and handles distributing broadband coming 
in, and

  Currently it has a cheap Belkin switch plugged into one of its ports, 
to give me additional ethernet connections, and three laser printers 
are plugged into the Belkin.  3 computers are plugged into the MacSense 
directly.

With something like this, I gladly pay extra for such a Mac-friendly 
unit. There was a time when this unit cost about $130 and the 
competition cost $70. And I think it was cheap at almost twice the 
competetion's price, because it was just so seamless to set up and 
never cost me an hour's lost time, or a morning, or a day.

Unfortunately, MacSense lost out in the ruthless battle by consumers to 
pay the absolute minimum possible.
