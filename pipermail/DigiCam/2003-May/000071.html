Sorry, I meant to send this earlier. This story comes from Adobe's web 
site. You have to register and get a free Snapfish account to submit 
your digital photos, shot only during the week of May 12-18. 
-----------------------------

Help tell the story of your community through digital images—and take 
part in one of the largest photography and publishing events in history.
For a single week—Monday, May 12, through Sunday, May 18, 2003—you can 
participate in a nation-wide project to capture a slice of American 
life through digital photography. The creators of the "A Day in the 
Life of America" series are back at work coordinating a publishing 
project that will result in 53 large-format volumes showing more than 
10,000 extraordinary digital photographs of a single week in America. 
The project will result in the largest number of trade-book titles ever 
published simultaneously.

How to participate
If you live in the US, have a digital camera, an email address, and a 
passion for telling stories with pictures, we invite you to take 
digital photographs from May 12-18, 2003 and submit them via the 
America 24/7 website (http://www.america24-7.com/index.shtml).
If the America 24/7 editors select one of your images, you will receive 
credit and see your work published alongside that of America’s top 
professionals. You will also receive one copy each of Adobe® Photoshop® 
Elements and Adobe Photoshop Album software, plus a copy of the book 
where your image appears.
Register now.

Adobe and America 24/7
Adobe is the official Publishing and Imaging Software Partner for this 
major publishing venture. Adobe InDesign® will be used to design and 
produce all of the books, and Adobe is also providing Adobe Photoshop, 
Adobe Acrobat®, and Adobe Illustrator® software, as well as Photoshop 
Elements and Photoshop Album to everyone whose work is published.
Check back soon for more updates, reports from the editing floor, and 
news as this exciting event draws closer.
