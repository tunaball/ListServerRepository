I have the Sony 707 and am very pleased with it.  It is excellent in 
low light.  Pictures taken indoors without flash, for example in a 
museum, have looked great.  I like the zoom (5x).  The time between 
pictures is a little disappointing if you are shooting action but it 
seldom affects me.  One of the features I really like is the ability 
to have the camera bracket the exposure and take 3 shots of every 
scene.

What I feel are shortcomings.
Time to focus is a little long - expecially when taking pictures of 
kids.  They have either moved and/or the picture becomes blurred. 
Perhaps I could fix some of that by taking the camera out of 
autofocus - dont' know yet.  This is my first automatic camera and I 
love it.  The camera has a lot of manual options but so far I haven't 
needed them.  It would be really nice to have a burst mode.

The closeup (macro) capability seems good.  I don't have much to 
compare it to but it is nothing I would brag about.

I know I focused on the shortcomings to help you make a decision. 
But I am very happy with the camera.
