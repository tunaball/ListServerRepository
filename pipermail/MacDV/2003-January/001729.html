Ron Woodland wrote:

> The beige G3 will accept a plug-in Firewire and/or USB card and work 
> just fine.  The PCI architecture used on that machine's motherboard is 
> version 2 of the standard.  My wife has a 300 MHz. model.  It has 
> extended the useful life of that machine another year or so.  I've 
> even tried capturing video on it, using a second Maxtor 60 GB hard 
> drive set up as a slave to the original, smaller hard drive (6 GB, I 
> think). Works great.  No dropped frames in either capturing or 
> printing to video.
>
> Older Macs, such as the 7100/7200/7500, 8500/8600, and 9500/9600 
> models also had PCI architecture, but they were built to version 1 
> specs.  They will not work with a Firewire/USB card.  You can install 
> the card, but the system will not be able to see it and the drivers 
> won't work.
>
> Ron Woodland
