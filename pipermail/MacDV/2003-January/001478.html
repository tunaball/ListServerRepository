Too bad you already bought a DVD burner. Formac sells a decent FW DVD 
burner with much improved DVD authoring software. $399 and works on a 
G3.

>I know Toast 5.1.1 will burn to DVDs, but is there a relatively simple way
>for me to take my iMovies, export them to a suitable format and then burn it
>to a DVD for viewing on my home DVD player?

Software Architects just released an updated application that will 
turn MPEG video into DVD-compliant files that Toast can burn to DVD. 
Then it should work like a standard video disc. Can't recall the 
name, and I think it is OS 9-only (don't know about Classic) but a 
good investment for $99.

>Monosyllabic answers are okay, seeing as I'm pretty much a desktop video

There are a whole host of Unixy utilities coming out that can be 
strung together to eventually produce a DVD, but based on what you 
say there, it might not be the solution you're looking for.
