Yeah, its already crashed on me the first time I played around with it 
while looking at the "Ken Burns" effect. It seems to be less stable 
than the last revision of iMovie 2. Some things I noticed right away 
that aren't bugs per-se, but are annoying:

  - there doesn't seem to be a way to change what columns from iTunes 
are displayed in the music panel. It would be nice to show the genre 
here.

- the contents of the iTunes libraries pull-down gets hosed if its too 
long and you pick items further down the list.

- there's no way to change the size of the movie preview window. I'd 
think this would be more of an issue for people with slower machines 
who would like to have a big iMovie window for the timeline and clip 
window, but minimize CPU load by having a small movie window

Some  good things I noticed:

  - the clip window seems much more well behaved as to leaving clips 
where you put them, and actually enlarging the clip space when you put 
clips on the bottom row.

  - the little scrubber pointers are there all the time, rather than 
having to go through clicking and dragging contortions to make them 
appear.

  - the "edit volume" feature is great

- the "zoom" slider is better than the old pull-down
