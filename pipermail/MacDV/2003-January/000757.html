Yes, I threw in the Sigma because of the imager, which I think would be

Just goes to show that digital still camera technology continues to evolve
at an astounding pace. Frankly, I'd never heard of the Sigma SD9 until just
prior to my post, when I wanted to check a reference to the Kodak DCS Pro
14n.

My (new) dream digital SLR:

  + 14 Megapixel
  + Full frame imager, 1:1 to 35mm frame
    (critical for wide angle work, virtual reality)
    [Note: this appears to be a major shortcoming of
    the Sigma SD9]
  + Foveon technology used in Sigma SD9
  + Built-in GPS, location meta data in each image
  + Canon or Nikon lenses, or comparable quality
  + Lens line that includes a 15mm rectilinear lens,
    or 14mm rectilinear, preferably.
  + Firewire
