I've had good luck with Sizzle. Its easy to use - the tricky part is 
getting the media in the right format.

I just played around with ffMpeg a bit today for the first time, and it 
looks very powerful, but I didn't have much luck with it. One problem I 
noticed and fixed is that it is based on unix macros which assume that 
you don't have any spaces in the paths to your home directory and to 
the media files you're working with. For example, if your User folder 
is named something like "John Doe", the ffMpeg engine installer won't 
work.
