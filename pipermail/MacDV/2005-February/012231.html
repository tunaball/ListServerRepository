This is a .Mac (dot-Mac) account. You put the pictures in your Public 
folder, make a photo page and Apple does the rest. For sharing of many 
photos, I use pBase though. $23 per year for 200 MB of storage vs $99 
per year from Apple. I didn't give up my .Mac account though. There are 
plenty of other reasons to keep it.

On pBase, it's much more tedious to set up and it's just photos, no 
slide shows or sharing files, movies, newsletters, etc. On dot-Mac you 
drag your photos into position; on pBase you change sequence numbers. I 
think both prices are fair for what you get. For example, I see that 
all three of the earlier GarageBand jam packs are sitting there for us 
to download free. I just started going through the new iLife 05 
tutorials last night.
