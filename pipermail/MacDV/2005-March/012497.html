Sony's newest - the HDR-FX1 ($3,700) - records in a format called HDV 
(1080i) on standard miniDV cassettes. Apple's newest versions of Final 
Cut Express and iMovie will capture and edit HDV, but currently Apple 
is suggesting that the finished HDV movie should be downsampled to SD 
for burning on a current DVD. Apple says that starting with HDV will 
produced higher resolution, better looking SD DVDs. I'm assuming we 
will be able to burn DVDs for full resolution on HDTV as soon as the 
industry settles the debate on standards (Blu-Ray against whatever the 
other format is). Wonder how many days it will take to burn an HD DVD? 
<g>

There's a huge monetary gap up to the next possibility. Apple's Final 
Cut Pro HD was designed to work well with footage from Panasonic's base 
HD camcorder - which I think starts at about $45,000.
