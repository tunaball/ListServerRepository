If you can find a used Panasonic GS400, that could be a good choice in 
your price range.  The GS400 was a more full-featured camera than the 
GS500 is, and had a list price of about twice as much.  I'm sorry that 
Panasonic took it off the market about a year ago, before they released 
the GS500.  I am very happy with our GS400.  It has a nice lens, good 
image stabilization, good sound, a good selection of features, and 
relatively few stupid design features that get in the way of using it 
effectively.

Analog pass-through is a feature that is far less common than it was a 
few years ago.  On the other hand, 3CCD camcorders are becoming more 
common in the lower end.  I don't know of a 3-chip camera that has 
analog pass through and a retail price under $700.  Perhaps others on 
the list can suggest something.  The only way to get those two features 
at that price may be buying used.
