I put together eight or nine-minute long docs, and would like to burn 
them to disk.  DVDs are still a little pricey - - so yesterday I 
burned one of my stories as a SuperVCD, to a CD-R.  It's not bad, if

be a better way.  After all, the CD-R can hold 700MB, and my

using TMPGENC, which stands for Tsunami MP Encoder.  TMPGenc enables 
you to adjust bitrate, quantaxation matrix, GOP structure, Interlace 
and many other parameters"

Maybe I barking up the wrong tree here, being very new at this.  I 
just want to burn the best quality video/audio I can, without wasting 
a big 4.7GB DVD on a nine-minute-long item.

wasted space) for a CD-R.

Avid XpressDV)
