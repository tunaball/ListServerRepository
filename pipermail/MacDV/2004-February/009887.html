You should be able to import directly into iMovie. Basically, the DV 
camcorder outputs Firewire just like it would if you were recording 
live from the camcorder on the Mac.

With my Canon ZR 40, there is a menu setting in the camera that puts 
it into pass-through mode. I remove any tape that might be in the 
camcorder (or just leave the tape door open). I flip the 
Camera/Off/Play(VCR) switch to Play(VCR). With iMovie running in 
camera mode, I start playing the VHS VCR that is attached to the 
camcorder input, and when the video appears in the iMovie window, I 
click iMovie's Import button.

Other DV camcorders may operate differently, so YMMV.

BTW, I believe that Costco currently is selling the ZR 60 (basically 
an improved version of the ZR 40) for somewhere around $375. There 
are Canon ZR 80/85/90 models, but I understand that they are missing 
some of the features of the ZR 60/65/70 series.
