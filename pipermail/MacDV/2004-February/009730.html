I have used 60 minute Fuji tapes and they are, in my opinion, as good 
as Sony but when you go to longer tape you have thinner tape that is 
more liable to have flaws and more likely to be damaged by camera's 
drive mechanism.  When you take this tape and record in LP mode the 
tape will theoretically have the same information recorded as it would 
at normal speed, but in reality with the longer tape having a higher 
incident of flaws you will almost certainly experience some/few/many? 
video dropouts. The other problem with LP mode is that the tape is 
moving slower so any flaws will be magnified eg. last 1 1/2 times 
longer.

If I was planning to do your project I would spring for the extra 
tapes, spend the time splitting the Hi 8 tapes over two DV tapes and 
look forward to less frustration down the road.
