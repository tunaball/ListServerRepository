Either two "clips" as you say (you can just save the first frame before
the movement starts) or check out a separate application that does allow
such holds. For example, Photo to Movie ($20) at lqgraphics.com is one
of the programs that gives you more control over your panning and
zooming. 

Another program is Slick Motion that is part of Volume 4 of Slick
Effects ($50) (at geethree.com. That one also lets you rotate. There are
other effects that come with this. 

I think it's worthwhile to have both of them.
