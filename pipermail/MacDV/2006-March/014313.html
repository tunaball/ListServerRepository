Roughly speaking, 'letterboxing' is filling with black lines the space created when the aspect ratio (width divided by height) of the video being input is different from the aspect ratio of the video being displayed or saved. Most commonly, black lines are added top and bottom to a video with 16:9 aspect ratio to give it a 4:3 aspect ratio for display on ordinary TV's.

If my definition of the term needs refinement, somebody else on the list will, I hope, let us know!
