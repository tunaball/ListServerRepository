To add to Ted Langdell's nice rundown of stills to FCP I have two 
suggestions:
1) Put a black sheet of construction paper on your scanner platten. 
This prevents any black type on the photo's reverse side from showing 
up subtly in the scanned image.

2) I am not sure about FCP but with Avids I used to scan at 720 x 540 
and then squeeze down to 720 x 486 in After Effects. This turns the 
square computer pixel into a pixel that will look normal when it is 
elongated vertically for TV. Easiest advice: do the circle test. Scan a 
round image and see what happens to it at 540 and at 486 when you put 
it on a NTSC monitor. Maybe you should use less than 540 for a 480 DV 
image.
