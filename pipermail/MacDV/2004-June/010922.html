> The reasoning regarding the use of a "breakout-box" to house the
> soundcard circuitry instead of just plugging into an available PCI slot
> is that the separate box can run more cooly than inside the computer
> with lots of heat sources.  The fear is that the heat will aversely
> affect the highly accurate clocks that are key in the analog to digital
> conversion.  The esoteric reasons given are that the external
> converters actually sound better.  I'm in no position to judge fine
> nuances in sound quality, but I do know that even the most benign noise
> reduction algorithm  punches big holes in anything recorded at CD
> quality (16 bit/44.1 kHz)  Not to mention the clocks used to support
> the built-in audio are cheap.  I've found the PCI card does a fine job
> for me.
