As simply a note, trying not to be too off topic: In DVD Studio Pro, 
this is extraordinarily easy to do, as even in the current version 
(sans interface) there are a couple of menus which have conditional 
"action" settings, such as, what to do when Track A is finished 
playing? Play Track A. This will loop forever, but then you can kill 
the loop by assigning the MENU button on the remote to take you to a 
menu. That sort of thing... and very easy. Corporate video people DO 
love this. Great for trade shows and lobby displays. The great thing, 
the next generation of DVD Studio Pro is only $499. I saw it at NAB and 
it is like iDVD doing a "Hulk" transformation.

> Try this:
>
> Create a new iDVD project.
>
> open customize drawer.
>
> Themes: (Old Themes) Set global theme.
> Settings:
> Set title to none
> Drag away the old to nothing.
> Drag your movie onto the background video
> Drag your movie onto the background audio
> Save your project.
> Quit iDVD.
>
> Pick your project.
> Right-click/show pkg contents
> open contents/resources
>
> Launch textedit
> Drag ProjectData onto textedit
>
> Search for MotionDuration:
> <key>Property persistent name</key>
> <string>MotionDuration</string>
> <key>Property type</key>
> <string>11</string>
> <key>Value</key>
> <string>30</string>
>
>
> ^^^--- change that 30 to 1800 (for 30 minutes = 60 * 30)
>
>
> Save.
>
> Launch iDVD. See if it works.
>
> Burn.
