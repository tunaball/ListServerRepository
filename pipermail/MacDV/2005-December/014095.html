Having gone through this with a number of my laserdisc titles that
aren't available on DVD, I've used two methods:

1) play the LD through the AV playthrough of my digicam into iMovie,
where I take care of the flip/change disc issues, then export out
through iDVD or DVDSP.

2) Play the LD through my EyeTV 200 and use the EyeTV software to deal
with the flip/change issues. Then export as elementary streams and
author with DVDSP (still using version 3, here). (Use the S-Video
connector if the LD player has an S-Video Out.)

Method 2 is much faster and easier (and requires far less free disk
space). I also don't see a quality difference. Thus, that is the method
I employ these days, but if you don't have an EyeTV 200, method 1 will
do the job for you.
