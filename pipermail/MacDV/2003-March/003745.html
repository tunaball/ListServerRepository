When a DVD player says that it can play VCDs, that does not mean it can 
necessarily play *homeburned* VCDs. It may be referring to 
commercially-pressed VCDs.

In order to play homeburned anything, the DVD player must have a 2nd 
laser than can read the dye change of homeburned discs rather than the 
pits of commercial discs.

As a rule of thumb, if your DVD player can play homeburned audio CDs, 
it should be able to play homeburned video CDs. Can your machine play 
homeburned Audio CDs?
