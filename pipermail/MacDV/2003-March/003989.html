There are basically two choices I think.

1. Quicktime ".mov" or .avi file with Cinepack compression. Cinepack 
has been around since Quicktime 2.0 (IIRC) and looks terrible, but for 
small screen sizes and ubiquitousness it can't be beat. Almost every PC 
since Win95 has Quicktime on board.

2. MPEG-1. This is probably the best thing to go with these days. You 
can get up to VHS quality with it, and any modern computer can play it 
(even if they don't have QT installed!).
