Agreed, unless you have access to a D/A converter and can
borrow someone's set-top DVD recorder (like the Panasonic,
Pioneer, etc lines).

Anyway, to respond directly to Mr. Ong:

1. You can use DVD Studio Pro to prepare the content and burn
    with toast. (Most DVD players want to see an empty Audio_TS
    folder too)
2. You can make two iDVDs like Jim suggests
3. You can export from iMovie through a Canopus/Sony/Whatever
    Digital to Analog converter to a set-top DVD recorder.
