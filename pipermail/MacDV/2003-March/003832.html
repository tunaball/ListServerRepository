Just take a sharp object and scratch the side with the coating.  On a 
CD-R, that is what actually contains the encoded data - the laser burns 
the reflective coating on the underside through the plastic disk 
material to create a null-reflection point representing a binary 1 (or 
0, I'm not sure).  Any significant amount of scratches to that 
reflective surface will probably make the disk unreadable.  I use a car 
key and do it several places hard until the reflective material flakes 
off, then simply throw it away.  There's no way anyone can read it.

Of course, in our world today you can't be too careful.  A CD shredder 
would be the ultimate protection.  Then again, I don't have anything 
archived that would be that sensitive.  My method is sufficient for the 
work I do.
