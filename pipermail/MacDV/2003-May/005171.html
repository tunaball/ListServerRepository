The closest you can get to "turning off extensions" in OS X, AFAIK, 
is to log in while holding down the shift key (start holding it down 
after typing the last character of the password but before hitting 
the Return key). This keeps non-essential stuff associated with the 
user from running at login (e.g., items in your Login Items System 
Preferences).

I believing holding down the shift key during a restart will have the 
same effect for non-essential processes that are global to all users.

I've gotten in the habit of logging in with the shift key before 
capturing DV from my camcorder in iMovie.
