>When it comes to writing scripts, I strongly recommend
>studying story structure, characterization, and dialogue.
>For learning about dialogue, listen to the way people
>speak in your life. Notice how sentences are broken,
>incomplete, and how people do not speak too
>directly. There is often a lot implied between the lines.
>Also, there's the basic rule that you should 'show, not
>tell' whenever possible. Instead of a character saying,
>'You've really pissed me off!', you have him slam his hand
>on the table and scowl.
