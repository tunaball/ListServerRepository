Yes.

You need to demux the file into separate audio and video tracks (that
will give you an m1v (video) and mp2 (audio) track.

Then, reencode the video to m2v (MPEG-2).

Finally, remux as SVCD-compliant MPEG-2.

You can use Cleaner, ffmpegX, or MissingMPEG Tools for these operations.
Personally, I would use ffmpegX (shareware) as it's a lot easier to use
than MissingMPEGTools (freeware) and a lot cheaper than Cleaner
(commercial).
