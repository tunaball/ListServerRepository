For others wanting help on editing a sound track in iTunes:
1.Control-click the track
2. Get info and choose options
3. Start and Stop the tune where you want.
4.Go to Advanced Menu. Click Convert Selection.

On Wednesday, November 10, 2004, at 10:59 PM, Lee Rose wrote:

> You don't have to extract the sound from a clip in the timeline. 
> Select the clip (or clips) you want the audio from, then go to 
> Edit>Share. Choose QuickTime, then Expert Settings. Make sure you 
> check Selected clips only. Then choose Export: Sound to AIFF and also 
> 41.1 Khz 16 bit stereo. The exported AIFF clip can be dragged into 
> iTunes directly, and then converted (if you choose) to any of iTunes 
> supported formats. (You can, of course, do audio editing in iMovie 
> before you Share. If you don't save the project, you won't keep the 
> edits in iMovie, if you don't want them.)
>
> Hope that helps
> Lee Rose
