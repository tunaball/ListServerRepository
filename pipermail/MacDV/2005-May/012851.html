On May 5, 2005, at 9:17 AM, macminialacool at mac.com wrote:

> It's a well known fact that we have had to be buying Quicktime Pro 
> keys since version 5.0 with a new key for every major release.

I don't know  if it was all that well known. Yes, I remember having to 
buy a new one for version 6, so I guessed one would probably be needed 
for version 7. Before that I don't remember having to buy one for major 
upgrades, and having to repurchase once isn't exactly a pattern -but it 
was a warning.

I think the biggest problem is the way QT Pro is upgraded. Its the only 
"paid" app where you can download an upgrade (either via software 
update or manually) and apply the upgrade prior to paying. What would 
happen if you could upgrade to the next major version of iLife via 
Software Update, but it crippled some of the features until you paid 
for the upgrade?

I think most people just expected a better warning about what would 
happening before they installed the update. Considering the unique 
nature of Quicktime/Pro I do.
