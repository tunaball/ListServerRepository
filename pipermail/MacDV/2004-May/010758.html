Do it all the time.  Our interview shows are recorded as separate 
streams onto to 3 macs with external drives.  Each records through a 
deck or media converter.  Regretfully, analog input is Y/C but our 
community media facility can work with it.  Each camera is linked to a 
hard drive and the combined streams are posted in Final Cut Pro.  There 
is only one audio recording.  The combined streams are edited like a 
banana.  Peeling away the top layers reveals the interviewer or another 
camera position.  When the edit is complete a self contained QuickTime® 
movie is made and the original media deleted.

Sounds like you just need to record one stream. Just plug your DV 
camera into a VCR output.  Use S if you can.  If you are using FCP then 
choose the "Non Controllable Device" setting in the log and capture 
window.

Expect to consume 25GB.
