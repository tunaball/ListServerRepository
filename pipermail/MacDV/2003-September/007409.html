Actually, the audio may be making the biggest difference in this case 
-- when exporting using one of the presets, it's probably compressing 
the audio, but when doing a "custom" export it's not.  5 min. of 
uncompressed stereo audio @ 16 bits/44khz will be about 50 MB.  The 
best compromise I've found is to use IMA 4:1 compression at 22khz 
sampling rate -- this will shrink the audio size to about 1/8 of the 
original size, while retaining pretty much of the sound quality (you 
could go mono to save even more space, but being a music video and all, 
I'd think you'd want to keep the stereo sound).

But then again, the MPEG-4 movie should have been *much* smaller as 
well, assuming the audio was compressed in the process.

One question -- were you exporting these files to the same hard drive?  
Is it formated for Mac OS Standard or Extended?
