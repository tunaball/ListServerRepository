On Dec 31, 2003, at 9:53 AM, James Asherman wrote:
>
> I could turn journalingg on. Is that a good thing?
> Jim

Yes.  You want journaling enabled on your filesystems in general.  It 
makes the FS more resilient in the event of unrecoverable I/O failure 
(like power off), and makes file system integrity repair much much 
faster.  fsck now does nothing except update the disk with uncommitted 
transactions.  Journaling doesn't improve performance, and may detract 
marginally, but the price is well worth paying.

It's great that Panther does journalling by default.
