Nero isn't bad, but lord is it Germanically complex!  Sort of like that 
button thing on the dash of a BMW 7 series -- intended to make 
operating all car functions simple, allegedly, but in reality the 
driver instead spends inordinate time rotating a knob and seeking 
functions!  Technology for its own sake!

Last year I had the pleasure of copying a series of Hong Kong soap 
operas onto DVD-R blanks with a NEC DVD reader, then latest Sony 
external FW/USB dual DVD-+RW, and the latest Nero.  It worked fine, but 
I had to mind my manners constantly.  Recently, using the ancient 
DVD-ROM in the Cube and my recently acquired Lacie external FW DVD+-RW, 
doing a few more transcriptions for my friend was not much more 
difficult than dropping in the master and the blank, and making one 
choice, in Toast Titanium 6.  A beautiful program -- but then, I am a 
died in the wool copyist, so I would probably lean to terming it the 
very best OS X program!

Don
