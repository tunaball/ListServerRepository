Sorry, I guess I should have known, from your original post, that English
is not a language you are fluent in.

"Umm, say again?" is a somewhat slangy way to say: I'm sorry, but I
didn't understand what you said. Could you look at what you said and try to
rephrase it in a more understandable way? Perhaps you didn't really mean
what you attempted to say, too.

It is not meant to be taken literally.

The reason I said it was that the sentence didn't make much sense to me.
You said: A few listers have had luck, so you should be okay.

a) "Luck" isn't sufficiently specific. I surmise, from what
follows, that you meant "good luck". But, except in cases like "you're in
luck", luck could mean bad or good, as opposed to "lucky", which normally
means "having good luck".

b) Logically, your statement is similar to saying "A few people have won
the lottery, so you should, too." Or perhaps better, "A few people have
survived fevers with temperatures over 112 degrees, so you should be ok."
Admittedly, more people play the lottery than are on this list, but the
fact that a "few" haven't yet burned up their cards is hardly an
indication that "you should be okay".
