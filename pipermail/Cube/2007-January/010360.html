I had one of those and smoked it when I tried plugging in my iPod, USB
mouse, and external hard disk.

FWIW - USB needs "smarts" on the computer side of it to figure out
just what you're plugging in and how to talk to it. To convert USB to
something else (say, Firewire), you'd pretty much need an entire
computer in the middle with a library of drivers to support all the
USB devices you might plug in.
I, too, was on a quest to get USB 2.0 (for a USB 802.11g adapter) and
eventually gave up and went with a network cable instead.
