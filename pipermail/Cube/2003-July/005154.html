> This doesn't sound like a power issue if everything else seems to 
> remain powered. It is likely that your three-ish year old hard drive 
> is failing - abnormal clicking is pretty simple to diagnose. Back your 
> data up, then back it up again. Make a backup of the backup. You can 
> get a large replacement drive on eBay pretty cheap (i got a 120 GB 
> Maxtor for $120). Be sure you get a 7200 RPM drive - your machine will 
> be noticeably faster than it is now. Furthermore, the silent beauty of 
> your cube has likely been overtaken by a semi-annoying whine from the 
> aging drive. A new hard drive will be whisper quiet.
