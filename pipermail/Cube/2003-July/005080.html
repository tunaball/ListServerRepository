>> I noticed someone in the group mentioned that a Geforce 4MX might 
>> work in the cube.  Has anyone successfully done this? If so, any 
>> temp readings? I've got an mac geforce 4mx card I would like to try 
>> but would like to reap the benefit of others experience if possible.
> 
> 4MX will work, but you would need to move the DC board and probably 
> to chip a bit of the card at the top. You would also need to modify 
> the bottom bracket. I also have one of these cards lying around, but 
> I don't think its performance justifies the trouble needed to put 
> that thing inside.
> I think one Cube owner did put it in, but I can't remember if it was 
> reported here on on cubeowner forums.
> As for the cooling, it _might_ work without a fan, but that would be 
> pushing the luck.

The benefit of it is not so much the performance for gaming and such; 
it's just that the 4MX has two digital outputs (one ADC, one DVI), so 
it would be great for anyone who wants to run two LCD monitors from 
their Cube.  I believe there is only one other card out there  -- the 
Radeon 9000, I think? -- with the ADC/DVI outputs, and it would also 
require mods and is much more expensive.
