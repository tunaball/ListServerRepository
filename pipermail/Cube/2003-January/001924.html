Yes, it absolutely is a matter of personal taste, and I certainly don't 
begrudge your choice. I was simply pointing out that, while it is an 
effective solution for you, I don't think it's something that will 
appeal to a great number of people.

By "defeat[ing] the whole point of the Cube," I meant that stringing an 
internal mechanism via IDE cable violates the elegance and compactness 
of the Cube, which were two of the Cube's big selling points—although 
admittedly, an external Firewire drive has a similar impact on the 
compactness score. And I don't think many would be comfortable running 
the Cube outside of its enclosure for an extended period of time. But 
it works for you, and that's really all that matters.
