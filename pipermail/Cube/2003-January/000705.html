Here are the details, direct from Robert a few months ago. I have reminded
him that there is still no comprehensive detail on their own site about the
program, but I suspect that will change soon with the new product listings
and site update.

"Upgrade program is difference in current list price. For example,
customer buys a single 1gig for $699 now. Then upgrades to a dual
1gig in 6 months. In six months, the dual 1gig might be $899, the
single 1gig $499. The upgrade price is therefore $899-$499=$400.
Trade up has to be a current model at the time of upgrade. Customer
buys direct (from PL) at list; customer sends old card back after new one is
installed; then we issue a credit when the working trade in arrives.
Customer pays freight for new card and return of old card."
