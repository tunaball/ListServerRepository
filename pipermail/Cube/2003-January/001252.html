I am an expert on this card for the Cube because I did a lot of 
research during the past year to find the fastest TwinView that would 
work in the Cube without a fan. I still have some for sale BTW. Anyone 
interested contact me off list.

In my humble opinion, you should install the 7500 WITHOUT the modified 
fence that attaches to the Cube's video port hole. Why? Because the 
7500 is plenty tight without it due to the combination of its connector 
and the screw in the upper rear corner. By skipping the fence, you 
allow a lot more air to enter directly into the heat sink area between 
the two video ports. Otherwise that opening is blocked by the fence and 
the card will run much hotter. Not installing the port fence aids in 
the natural convection cooling of the card.

You certainly do not need any fan inside the Cube as long as you 
install it without the air flow blocking fence. That's why it's the 
perfect TwinView video card for the Cube. It is the fastest TwinView 
video card that will continue to run in the Cube without a fan.

Now this thing about running the 7500 without the fence is my own bias. 
I learned this approach from someone else on this list who was doing it 
long before me. It's really a common sense thing. I am certain that 
this is the best way to make sure the card runs cooler. Some list 
members swear by "the fence must be installed" point of view. I don't. 
So before I get attacked for telling you this is the only way, I am 
telling you this is my opinion of how best to run your card. I am only 
speaking for myself and others like me who run the card this way.
