Hard Disk Toolkit can both "add" and "resize" partitions, since at 
least version 2.0x, which is 5-6 years old.

To increase (or decrease) the number of volumes HDT's scheme is to 
delete partitions and then create new partitions. So you have to 
either have an empty partition, that you've copied everything off, or 
a current backup of it if you want that data back.

I don't think you can resize running HDT off your startup Drive. And 
HDT requires its drivers to be installed in order to change volume 
quantities or resize partitions. Unless you have the newest version 
of HDT, your OS X drivers will probably be much more up-to-date. 
Depending on your mix CCC/reformatting may be easier.

Even if you have newest HDT and install its drivers then decide to go 
back to Apple's drivers, you could hose your drive. Been there. Done 
that.

CCC is a godsend, if you have the space available to temporarily 
relocate stuff  ...
