The movie is here.  However, it was a LITTLE harder than shown here.
one of the screws is obstructed so that you cant get the driver in a
straight line with it (unless you have one of those right angle  
drivers).

So you have to disassemble the cube chasis a little bit more than is  
indicated here.  once you have the cube out of the enclosure and
see the screws it will be obvious what needs to be removed.  it's pretty
easy.

http://www.info.apple.com/usen/cip/html/g4cube/g4cube-harddrive- 
cip.mov.html

On Tuesday, January 28, 2003, at 11:40  AM, James Knight wrote:

> Hi Daniel
>
> It's pretty straightforward.
>
> From memory...
>
> Take the core out of the cube and identify the hard disk. Remove the 2
> cables connected to it. You might have to lift the airport card flap  
> to do
> so.
>
> Look for the 3 screws which hold the hard disk on to the heat sink -  
> they
> are all in a line. Unscrew them and draw the hard disk out.
>
> Remove the plastic rail from the other side of the hard disk and  
> attach it
> to the new one.
>
> Make sure that the jumpers on the hard disk are set to 'master'.
>
> Slide the new drive in and attach the three screws through the  
> heatsink.
>
> Replace the cables.
>
> Put the core back in the cube.
>
> Have fun
>
> HTH
