Hi Don,

I thought of this a year ago, and have attempted prototypes on two 
occasions. The issue is simply that there's no way to low-volume 
fabricate the part, as described. We cannot injection mold the piece, 
as doing so would require something around US$40,000 in tooling costs. 
We can't bend it to shape, as doing so causes bubbles and other defects 
to appear (unavoidable) in the corners. And, even if we could somehow 
overcome the heat-caused defects in the corners, we would than have to 
glue on the top... which creates another set of ugliness problems.

In other words, to make this thing acceptably pretty, we have to mass 
produce it. But, there's not a market for anything like the several 
thousand pieces we would have to sell to make it a reasonable business 
risk. I am, for instance, only now down to my last 10 of the 150 piece 
run we did of the DVCube stand. I think the "slice of bread" stand 
would perhaps sell 300 to 600 pieces, total.

We're stuck with items for the Cube that can be economically built 
using small shop/hand methods... or, no Cube products at all.

Sad, but true... as I would truly like to own a couple of the stands we 
are discussing, myself.

Thank you so much for your awareness of MacMice and your love for the 
Cube.

Take care.
