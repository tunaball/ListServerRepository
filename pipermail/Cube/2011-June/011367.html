Hello!

I've finally found a Cube suitable for me, and now I'd like to introduce 
myself!

I've been active for 2+ years on the LEM G-List with my Power Mac desktop 
computers. I have them in my cellar, and getting to work with the Power Macs 
has become complicated. So, for a year or so, the desire to get a Cube was 
developing and I was watching eBay for quite some time to get one that is 
cheap, yet in healthy working order!
The Cube is now on top of my Linux PC in the home office.

I've bought it for around 60 Euros, and it is in good optical condition. It 
came only with the external PSU, nothing else.
I replaced the original 30 GB HDD with my 300 GB HDD. I use a modified 
KeyLargoATA.kext that ignores the missing lba-48 Open Firmware property, thus 
I have access to areas beyond 128 GB, which is one big partition labled 
“Data”. Inside the 128 GB frame I have partitions for Mac OS 9 (“Classic”), 
Mac OS X 10.2 Jaguar, 10.3 Panther, 10.4 Tiger and 10.5 Leopard. When spare 
time permits I will also install Jaguar, Panther and Classic.

For working I use Tiger and Leopard, although I noticed that 10.4 just rocks 
even with as little as 450 MHz, whereas 10.5 feels a little slow, especially 
with the graphical feedback.

The Cube has an ATI Radeon 7200 installed, it is listed as “Rage6” in System 
Profiler. It has a fan.

I upgraded to 3×512 MB PC100/PC133 RAM modules giving it its maximum of 1.5 GB 
of RAM.

The optical drive is not recognized at all. Maybe it is a loose connector or 
so, I'll check this later. I don't really use CDs and DVDs all that much 
anymore.
The disc that I put in it is stuck for now, as I don't know how to get it 
ejected without a working eject key. (The key is working alright, but the 
drive is not recognized… So it isn't doing anything…)

I'm planning on picking up some USB speakers, preferably the original Apple 
Pro Speakers, but this is a matter of how much they cost and how much I'm 
willing to pay. And I'm willing to pay as little as 20 Euros, but not > 50 
Euros as they are sold on eBay these days.
