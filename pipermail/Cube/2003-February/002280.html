> More nerdy babbling about the PS:
>
> One more oddity of the Cube's bizarre design. The external power supply
> apparently needed a gizmo on the output side to give it a polled 2 watt
> load. Under no-load conditions the pulse increases to infinitely wide 
> and
> the magnetics saturate and burn out the supply.
> This is not an issue with other designs(including PC's) because the PS 
> is
> always connected to a minimum load. If you unplug the load and run the 
> PS
> you run the risk of burning it out.
> The Cube's PS had to be designed to be potentially unloaded(unplugged) 
> so
> the upshot is that the discrepancy is not in the Cube itself but in 
> the PS.
>
> It looks like the 11-12 watt "no-load" load is what Apple decided is
> necessary to keep the pulse from overmodulating with the variance a 
> result
> of periodic polling the Cube to decide if it is plugged in or not.
>
> Hope that makes sense . . .
