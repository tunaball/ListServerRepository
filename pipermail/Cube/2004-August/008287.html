> Actually, all you really _need_ to listen to music anywhere is the wireless 
> card in whatever machine you want to control iTunes with and the Airport 
> Express near the stereo you want to pipe the music to.
> That said, an iBook without wifi is rather like a Sundae without chocolate - 
> you're missing the best part of a laptop!

You don't even need that. You do need _some_ wireless transmitter 
connected to the computer that you are streaming from, but it doesn't need 
to be internal. You'll get better throughput with a third-party 11g unit,
and probably better range, as well. And for less money, if you shop 
wisely.

>
> You'll want the older Airport card for the Cube (not Airport Extreme). OWC 
> had a batch on discount a few months ago for $60 or so each. No idea if they 
> still have them. They're pretty hard to find these days.
>
> Unfortunately, my personal experience with the Cube and Airport Express 
> hasn't been that good. I've got two Airport Expresses at opposite ends of the 
> house and the Cube almost in the middle. Even though the Cube reports full 
> signal strength, I still get audio dropouts on both units. They're much worse 
> in the living room next to the big stereo & TV setup (lots of electronics) as 
> opposed to the bedroom, which only has a small stereo. In all other aspects, 
> the Airport Express rocks. Setup was a breeze and all of my wifi devices work 
> well moving between the two units.
