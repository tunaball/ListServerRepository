I'm sorry, but this is so wrong.
'current' in Amperes in not something that is produced and then 'sent
out' to the connected devices. It is a capacity of the power source to
provide current to the connected devices. So you can power a device that
draws 50 mA with a power supply rated for 50 A, and there is no chance
to 'fry' the device.
Vise versa is possible though - the device that draws too much current
can fry the power supply that is not able to deliver.
