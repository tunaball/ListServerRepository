I made a nice wooden box that was sturdy enough to hold the display 
securely on the bottom. The inside dimensions were just large enough 
to surround the plastic, with the third leg removed. I then cut an 
aperture just large enough to expose the screen. It all fit snugly 
with the help of a small piece of sponge rubber at the back of the 
display. I drilled holes in the bottom and the top for ventilation, 
painted it flat black, mounted the speakers on each side and attached 
it to my wall. It looked great, almost like a well mounted and framed 
photograph, and worked well for several months. However, in the end I 
decided it just wasn't convenient enough to use and it went back to 
my desk top with the third leg re-attached. No modifications of any 
kind were made to the display or speakers.
