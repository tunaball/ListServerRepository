     Never use tap (or shower head) water, just distilled water, the 
fewer minerals (which can be conductive as well as sticky), the 
better. The lukewarm water in your shower probably included (if 
Russian plumbing is anything like in the US) hot water that came down 
copper pipes, and so includes copper sulfate in small quantities. If 
your municipal water is treated as it is in the US, you have all 
sorts of other chemicals that were added to kill microbes, &c. 
Supposedly harmless to humans in small doses, but they're still 
electrolytes.
