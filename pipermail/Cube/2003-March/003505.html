On 3/26/03 10:43 AM, rod at eslickdesign.com added a note to the human
symphony, when this was said:

> 
> A quick (and perhaps silly) question-the 64meg chip which is installed
> on the cube can be removed and replaced with a larger stick?

Yes, it can be. You can put a 512MB stick in each slot, for a max of 1.5 GB.

> Also, it does not hurt performance to have ram sticks of 256/128/128
> (for example) installed does it?

Nope, doesn't hurt - although by replacing what's there with anything other
than a 512, you're kind of wasting the slots. Although RAM isn't as cheap as
it was a year ago, it's still pretty good (512's are about $70 or so at OWC
for example), and you'll get a much better bang for the buck if you install
a 512 in one or more slots (all of them if you can swing the dough).
