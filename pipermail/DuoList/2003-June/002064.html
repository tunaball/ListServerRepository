It depends very highly on needs. For basic e-mail/browsing/WPing/etc the 
280c is often quite a bit faster. 280c is also noticeably faster in general 
Finder operations, even with the 2300 running a more PPC-native OS wil 8.6. 
With a bit of work, you can get a 280c running System 7.1 that boots in 
3-3.5 megs of RAM and has most/all of the capabilities of the later ones. 
On my 280c I've had iCab, Eudora, Appleworks 5, a game of Solitaire, AOL 
Instant Messenger, and an IRC client open at the same time without a 
problem, with a total of 32 megs of RAM. About the only reason the 2300 is 
better is it'll run the latest IE, but iCab mostly negates that and IE 5.x 
is slow enough on the thing that it's painful.

Anyway, just my humble opinion :) Unless you need some PPC app the 280c 
tends to be overall faster.
