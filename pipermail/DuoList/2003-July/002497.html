I'm not sure what you mean by "DNS servers are free to all."  When you
register your domain name and point it to a static IP address, your domain
'foo.net' and its corresponding IP address is propogated throughout the net
by the registrar.  Anything else, however, is up to you.  If you  want
'www.foo.net' or 'ftp.foo.net' to resolve, you'll have to provide that
yourself. Right?

Additionally, a DSL connection requires termination on the other end.  When
you said you have Qwest passthrough service, who is terminating the other
end of the DSL line?  Does Qwest provide termination right onto the Internet
backbone or do you have some other service provider terminating your DSL line?

-tom
