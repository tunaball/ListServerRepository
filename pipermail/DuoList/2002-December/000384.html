> measurements given in millimeters are for drive height or thickness. 
> Newer

David,

	Someone much smart than me on this list taught me this trick:

	Zip tie the old brackets to the new thinner hard drive just where 
they would go if the holes actually lined up (same position as the 
thicker older drive you are replacing).  Then stick that drive in the 
machine and enjoy you new bigger hard drive.  My 2300 has a ten gig hard 
drive installed this way and it has survived for a couple of years 
including a hard three foot drop to a carpetted floor.  Others on the 
list have done the same without difficulties.

Cool cheap trick ;-).
