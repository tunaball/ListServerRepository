Hello,

These are just hints:

1. Them white (or other) plastic sucking-cup hangers that sometimes sit on the tiling in
peoples bathrooms/kitchens. For the most part they act as hangers for towels, brushes etc.
The "hooks" differ: some have a wider bend than others, and would be useless. One variety has
a flexible narrow bend which should keep an ethernet cable still if clasped onto something
flat & clean. That may of course be a problem in itself...

2. Just may be that you could get a stretch of double-action adhesive to line some length of
the cable. Who knows?
