> 	Mr. G. is correct here when stating System 7 has problems with HFS+ 
> partitions but I'd like to add one detail.  What he's talking about 
> will happen if you're trying to mount the hard disk locally (i.e., on 
> the Duo's SCSI port).
>
> 	Since, as I understand it, Mr. Santos, you wish to connect the two 
> devices using your LAN, then it will work.  There is one caveat, 
> though. System 7 can only see file names of up to 31 characters in 
> length and under 2 GB in size.  If you have files on the eMac longer 
> and larger than that, you will have problems.
>
> 	Why does it work?  Because the Duo won't be trying to access the 
> filesystem directly.  It will be going through the eMac's AppleTalk 
> mini-server and all the file requests will be translated by the eMac 
> to its hard disk and back to the Duo.  The only thing the AppleTalk 
> peer-to-peer network won't do is make it possible for the Duo to SEE 
> all characters past the 31 character limit (but you can still work 
> with 'em) or manipulate files larger than 2GB (can't work with 'em at 
> all).  Those are operating system specific limitations on the Duo 
> (System 7).
