CDR is cheap, and not so limiting on the Mac (it is on a PeeCee....can you 
say 120 gig hard drive???!) as most 2300s (such as mine) use 1-2 gig HDs.  Back 
up the data you need, or mirror the HD onto a CD if less than 700 megs of 
data.  You will need a DuoDock or a MiniDock to connect the drive, along 
w/appropriate cables.

Alternatively, if you have another Mac, AppleTalk them together and backup 
the data files onto the other unit (my desktop has the CDRW; I back up most data 
on that drive).

Tape drives work well, too.  I've got a DAT drive I'm not using if anyone is 
interested...HP mechanism, holds like 6 gigs.  Retrospect does a fine job.  
Got two drives, I'm not giving up on DATs.
