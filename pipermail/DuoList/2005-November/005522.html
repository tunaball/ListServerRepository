It's called "Iomega driver." and  there's probably no easy way for 
you to get it on your computer if you can't even get your stuff off.

But the driver is not necessarily needed as long as everything's on 
when you start up. (not restart) If the disk shows up on your 
desktop, OK. If not, you might need to run a repair program over the 
zip disk on another computer first, or use a different disk.

If it doesn't mount, you could try booting up with extensions off 
(shift key down) while everything is hooked up and the disk is in, 
then looking for any application with the words, "disk, mount, or 
scsi" in the name, to mount it.

(make sure the drive is on before you put the zip disk in, and 
sometimes the zip drive needs to be disconnected from power between 
attempts)
And that file that freezes everything up when you click on it? Well 
don't click on it. ;-)


Good luck
Roger
