Hello again, I found what caused the problem at last.

After looking into the screen and not finding any signs och damage or 
so, i began thinking.

The image had 5 columns and 2 rows of distorted pattern, and ii didn´t 
match the connectors of the screen; 8 at the bottom and 4 at the side. 
Probably not them.

But it did match the pixel screen size: 5 columns X 128 = 640! Sound 
digital to me, so maybe the error was to found in a circuit or so.

At that time I remembered that i had a screenproblem when inserting a 
Vram card into my DuoDock, and the screen got bad. And I found the 
corrupted ram, put it in the machine and a similar distorted pattern 
shows up.

So it was probably a motherboard/memory problem then!

I examined the motherboard at close range and strong light, and found 
it; a place where a pair of "legs" from a chip where physically 
damaged. One of the support-pinns on the back-cover, the grey plastic 
ones, had rubbed un those "legs" and finally caused a shortage.

I cleaned up the area whith a scalpell and some 
electronic-cleaning-spray, carved some of the support away, and put 
thing together agian. Voila; it worked as nothing ever happend!
