You sure aren't getting much help here. I think I lost the original 
post, so I don't know who you are, but I would suggest pulling out 
the CF card and trying to start with a floppy. If it won't start from 
HD or floppy it could be a bad PRAM battery, (which is a pain to get 
to as I recall). Try zapping the PRAM. You may have to search for the 
correct key combo, but it is probably "control, option, p, r", 
holding these down upon startup until you hear two or three startup 
chimes, then release and see if it boots.
