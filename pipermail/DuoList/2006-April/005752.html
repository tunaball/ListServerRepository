Fabian,
If you do not have a HD caddy you will need to get one you will also need a 
ribbon and power connection (cable extension and or I connection) if you do 
not already have them.  Gawd it's been so long since I have been in a Duo 
Dock.
>From what I do remember, Hard Drive size is dependent on OS and you can even 
put in 2 HD's if you like, I did in mine.   Any Older SCSI HD will work 
(meaning most SCSI desktop  HD's).  I had one 10 gig and 1 20 gig. It's easy 
to do.  But you should be running 7.1 or higher if you have a 280 or 2300 it 
will be more fun since they can run 8.1.  The higher the OS the bigger the 
HD.
