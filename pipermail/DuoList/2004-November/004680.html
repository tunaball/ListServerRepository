Hmmm...think that is a dte to dte cable...you may need a "null modem" adapter 
on the 9 pin side to turn it into a dte/dce cable.  Best bet is a true "modem 
cable."  Used to build them myself, a google search should give several hits 
on the pin connections.  As for the modem itself, any external (and many PC 
card modems) are true "intelligent" modems.  The internals (as they reside on 
the data bus) can use the microprocessor to do a lot of the data conversion, 
etc.  Thus the earlier comment...call 'em "dumb" vs "smart" modems.  Any ext. 
modem has to have its own microP, as the serial link is not fast enough (std. 
serial port, not USB2) to support such data handling.
