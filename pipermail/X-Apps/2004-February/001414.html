As others have suggested, Distiller (now available only with Acrobat 
Pro) does make smaller pdfs from text saved as PostScript. (It's 
puzzling that Acrobat Pro can't do this itself.)

But if you're scanning a document, unless you subsequently use OCR to 
turn it into text, you're dealing with large graphics, not text. So 
shrinking the size of the pdf will depend directly on how much you can 
degrade the graphic and still read it. You may get some saving by 
making sure you're using grayscale and not full color.

My experience with making copied articles into pdfs was that they were 
always huge.
