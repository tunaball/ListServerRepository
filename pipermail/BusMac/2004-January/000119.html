I'd recommend you also look at Document Express 
(www.documentexpress.com). This is a deceptively powerful tool that not 
only handles direct email campaigns, but can also be used to automate 
email replies for the campaign or webforms. It works with your existing 
mail servers/programs and databases so you don't have to change your 
existing workflow. It just inserts itself where appropriate to act as a 
parser and "traffic cop" to manage your email traffic.

Good luck,
Dexter

> I have a couple of recommendations ...
>
> A true list management program that is rock-solid is LetterRip Pro.  
> It was
> developed by the developers of Entourage and Claris eMailer.  It's not 
> free
> and it might offer more than you need for a periodic announcement.  
> There is
> a free trial period. <www.letterrip.com>.  LetterRip will allow you to 
> set
> up discussion lists as well (if that is of interest).
>
> I also use  Intellimerge from Intelli Innovations </www.intellisw.com>.
> This software is designed for sending announcement style emails and 
> allows
> your to insert custom fields for greetings, personalized numbers, etc. 
>  I
> import my email address list and other information in each week for a
> mailing I make to several hundred folks - you could do so with your
> FileMaker database.  It sends individual emails and will handle 4,000 
> very
> easily.
>
> Both are OS X and might still be available in OS 9.
