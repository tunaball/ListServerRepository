Good idea on the introductions.

Arakawa is a supplier of hardware for hanging "things" from very small 
steel cable.  Shelves, signs, art, etc.  We use BizBasics (Filemaker 
Pro templates for Customer Service, Mail order, Inventory) and 
Filemaker Server, QuickBooks, iPhoto for tracking photos of user 
installations.  Have a 10 user mac-only network, with a linux server 
running filemaker databases (about 40 different ones, almost 1 GB of 
data).

Most of my questions tie in to using BizBasics, or finding cheap and 
effective ways of sharing information within a networked office 
environment.  For example, we figured out how to create private and 
shared calendars for all employees using iCal and one .mac 
subscription, so that we have a shared calendar server so we all know 
each other's schedules.

Not very pleased with QuickBooks, but haven't heard any convincing 
evidence that other accounting software apps are better.
